 8.  Public hearings.

a.

Public hearing and Council action on a request from Maria Melendez De Reyes, applicant, for consideration of a Special Use Permit pursuant to City Code Section 110-3.5.5 to allow a Family Home Daycare with up to 12 children maximum in the RH zoning district on the premises known as 3633 Heritage Ln and more particularly described as Tax Map Parcel(s) 58-1-15-012.

 

b.

Public hearing and Council action on a resolution to move forward with next stage of funding for engineering of the proposed University Drive Extension project.

 

Approved, unanimously, the request of Maria Melendez De Reyes, applicant, for consideration of a special use permit pursuant to City Code Section 110-3.5.5 to allow a family home daycare with up to 12 children maximum in the RH Zoning District on the premises known as 3633 Heritage Lane and more particularly described as tax map parcel(s) 58-1-15-012, subject to the following conditions:

            1.         The applicant shall care for a maximum of twelve children;

            2.         No more than two employees, exclusive of the applicant, shall be permitted during                                         the hours of childcare use;

            3.         Two of the driveway spaces shall be maintained for parking of two automobiles for                                           the residential use, and the residential use shall be limited to a maximum of two                                      vehicles on the premises;

            4.         No signage pertaining to the child care business shall be permitted on the premises;

            5.         The outdoor play area shall not be used for playing prior to 8:00 AM;

            6.         There shall be no modifications to the exterior of the building which detract from                                                the residential character of the site;

            7.         The Special Use Permit shall be valid only for the applicant and shall not be                                                    transferrable to any other person;

            8.         The applicant shall obtain and maintain all licenses required by the state to operate;                             and

            9.         The Special Use Permit shall be valid for a period of 2 years. Prior to expiration,                                       City Council may extend the period of validity.
 
 

b.

Public hearing and Council action on a resolution to move forward with next stage of funding for engineering of the proposed University Drive Extension project.

 

Approved, 5-1, to defer Council action and discussion on a resolution to move forward with next stage of funding for engineering of the proposed University Drive Extension project to January 24, 2017. (Councilmember Schmidt opposed.)
 
 
 
 9.  Items not requiring a public hearing.

Presentation of the City of Fairfax FY 2016 Comprehensive Annual Financial Report (CAFR) and the Auditor’s Communication with Those Charged with Governance Letter.

 

A representative from Cherry Bekaert presented the City of Fairfax FY 2015 Comprehensive Annual Financial Report (CAFR) and the auditor’s communication with those charged with governance letter.
 
 

b.

Consideration of a resolution to rename the Ratcliffe-Allison House as the Ratcliffe-Allison-Pozer House.

 

Approved, unanimously, a resolution re-naming the Ratcliffe-Allison house as the “Ratcliffe-Allison-Pozer House” in recognition of Kitty Barrett Pozer’s role in preserving the house and her many contributions to the City of Fairfax spanning several decades.
