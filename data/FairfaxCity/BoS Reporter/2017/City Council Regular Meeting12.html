City of Fairfax, Virginia 22030-3630

                     COUNCIL ACTIONS OF
                         June 27, 2017 
 
 1.  Call the regular meeting to order.
 
 2.  Invocation (Councilmember Miller).
 
 3.  Pledge of Allegiance.
 
 4.  Presentations and Proclamations.

a.

Presentation of a proclamation declaring June as LGBTQ+ Pride Month in the City of Fairfax.

 

b.

Presentation of a proclamation commending the City of Fairfax Museum and Visitor Center on their 25th Anniversary.

 
 
 5.  Presentations by the public on any item not subject to a public hearing.
 
 6.  Adoption of the agenda.
 
 7.  Consent agenda.

a.

Introduction of a Supplemental Appropriation Resolution in the amount of $34,612 for the transfer of C&I funds from the Transportation Tax Fund to the Capital Fund to fund preliminary engineering for the Judicial Drive Trail Connection Project.

 

b.

Introduction of a supplemental appropriation resolution in the amount of $500,000 for the transfer of Revenue Sharing and C&I funds from the transportation Tax Fund to the Capital Fund for the remaining project costs on the Burke Station Road Project.

 

c.

Consideration of a resolution to endorse the City’s project submission for the I-66 Outside the Beltway Concessionaire Funding and authorize the execution of a letter of support for Fairfax County’s Jermantown Road bridge expansion project submission.

 

d.

Consideration of a Modification to an Awarded Contract to Volkert, Inc. for Construction Management for the Kamp Washington Project.

 

Approved, unanimously, the consent agenda items.

 
 
 
 8.  Public hearings.

a.

Public hearing and Council action on an ordinance authorizing the City Manager to execute a Deed of Lease for a term beginning July 1, 2017 and ending June 30, 2019 by and between the City of Fairfax, Virginia, and Main Street Child Development Center, Inc., for approximately 7,364 square feet of space in the former Green Acres Elementary School, located at 4401 Sideburn Road.

 

Approved, unanimously, an ordinance authorizing the City Manager to execute a deed of lease by and between the City of Fairfax, Virginia and Main Street Child Development Center, Inc. for premises located at 4401 Sideburn Road (a portion of the former Green Acres Elementary School.)
 
 
 
 9.  Items not requiring a public hearing.

a.

Consideration to adopt the 2017 Emergency Operations Plan for the City of Fairfax.

 

Approved, unanimously, to adopt the 2017 City of Fairfax Emergency Operations Plan.
 
 

b.

Consideration to adopt a resolution declaring support for and commitment to the overall goals of the Paris Climate Agreement.

 
Approved, 5-1, a resolution declaring support for and commitment to the overall goals of the Paris Climate Agreement. (Councilmember Schmidt opposed.)
 
 

c.

Consideration of a resolution approving a plan of lease financing for the Fire Station 33 replacement project.

 

Approved, 4-2, a resolution approving a plan to replace Fire Station 33 to include the authorization of the proposed lease financing plan. (Councilmembers Miller and Schmidt opposed.)
 
 
 
 10.  Approval of minutes.

a.

June 13, 2017 Regular Meeting.

 
 
 11.  Closed meeting for consultation with legal counsel.
  • Approved, unanimously, to authorize and approve the settlement, on the terms and conditions outlined by the City Attorney in closed meeting, of City of Fairfax v. Bank of America, N.A. (Fairfax County Circuit Court NO. 2016-13666), relating to the acquisition of certain right of way from the defendant as part of the Northfax Project. Approved, unanimously, to authorize the City Attorney to finalize the negotiations and any settlement agreement in this matter and to authorize the City Attorney, the City Manager and the Mayor, as applicable, to execute any documentation necessary to finalize the settlement and to conclude the court case.
 
 
 
 13.  Council Comments.
 
 14.  Adjournment.

a.

Presentation of the draft TransAction Plan by the Northern Virginia Transportation Authority.

 

b.

Discussion of the two-year transportation project priority list.
