a.

Public hearing and Council action on a request from the Moore Family Child Care, applicant, by Daphne Bowers Moore, Owner/Agent, for consideration of a Special Use Permit pursuant to City Code Section 110-3.5.5 to allow a Family Home Daycare with up to 12 children maximum in the RH zoning district on the premises known as 3631 Heritage Lane and more particularly described as Tax Map Parcel(s) 58-1-15-013.

 

Approved, unanimously, the request of Moore Family Child Care, applicant, by Daphne Bowers Moore, owner/agent, for consideration of a special use permit pursuant to City Code Section 110-3.5.5 to allow a family home daycare with up to 12 children maximum in the RH Zoning District on the premises known as 3631 Heritage Lane and more particularly described as tax map parcel(s) 58-1-15-013, subject to the following conditions:

  1. The applicant shall care for a maximum of twelve children;
  2. Unless the applicant can clearly demonstrate to Zoning Administration, prior to its approval, that an onsite or offsite parking arrangement would support additional employees, the employees allowed to work at the home daycare shall be limited to those that reside on the premises;
  3. During the hours of operation, two driveway parking spaces shall be available for dropping off or picking up children. The applicant shall maintain a parking agreement for two spaces with the neighboring Country Club Hills Recreation;
  4. No signage pertaining to the child care business shall be permitted on the premises;
  5. The outdoor play area shall not be used for playing prior to 8:00 AM;
  6. There shall be no modifications to the exterior of the building which detract from the residential character of the site;
  7. The Special Use Permit shall be valid only for the applicant and shall not be transferrable to any other person;
  8. The applicant shall obtain and maintain all licenses required by the state to operate; and
  9. The Special Use Permit shall be valid for a period of five years. Prior to expiration, City Council may extend the period of validity.
 
 

b.

Public hearing and Council action on an ordinance amending Chapter 102 (Utilities) of the Code of the City of Fairfax, Virginia, by adding a new charge to Article II (Fees and Charges).

 
Approved, unanimously, an ordinance amending the Code of the City of Fairfax, Virginia, Chapter 102 (Utilities), Article II, Section 102-33 by adding a new wastewater lateral repair and replacement charge. He further moved the ordinance to become effective April 1, 2017.
