City of Fairfax, Virginia 22030-3630

                     COUNCIL ACTIONS OF
                         May 3, 2017 

1.

Discussion and mark-up of the City’s proposed FY 2017-18 Budget.

 
 
   SPECIAL MEETING - 7:00 P.M.

1.

Call the special meeting to order.

 

2.

Adoption of the agenda.

 

3.

Public hearings.

 

a.

Public hearing and Council action on an ordinance amending Chapter 82 (Streets, Sidewalks and other Public Places), Article I of the Code of the City of Fairfax, Virginia, to add new provisions relating to minor encroachments into City rights of way (neighborhood entry signs, landscaping and similar appurtenances).

 
Deferred, unanimously, to the May 9, 2017, City Council meeting.
 
 

b.

Public hearing and Council action on an ordinance amending and readopting Chapter 90, Article II, Division 1, Section 90-33 of the Code of the City of Fairfax, Virginia, pertaining to classification and taxation of certain real property in the City of Fairfax used for or zoned for commercial and industrial purposes, and to establish a separate tax rate on such real property effective as of January 1, 2017, revenues from which would be used exclusively for transportation purposes that benefit the City of Fairfax.

 
Approved, unanimously, an ordinance amending and readopting Chapter 90, Article II, Division 1, Section 90-33 of the Code of the City of Fairfax, Virginia, pertaining to classification and taxation of certain real property in the City of Fairfax used for or zoned for commercial and industrial purposes and such ordinance shall include a tax rate for such real property for calendar year 2017 at $0.105 per $100 of assessed value, revenues from which to be used exclusively for transportation purposes that benefit the City of Fairfax.
 
 

c.

Public hearing and Council action on an ordinance amending Chapter 102, Article II, Section 102-31 of the Code of the City of Fairfax, Virginia to increase the rates for wastewater service furnished by the City by 10.0% and to delete references to water service rates.

 
Approved, unanimously, an ordinance amending Chapter 102, Article II, Section 102-31 of the Code of the City of Fairfax, Virginia to increase the rates for wastewater service by 10.0% effective for bills rendered on or after July 1, 2017, and deleting references to rates for water service furnished by the City.
 
 

d.

Public hearing on the proposed FY18 Budget.
*No council action*

 

4.

Items not requiring a public hearing.

 

a.

Consideration of the City’s proposed FY 2017-18 Budget.

 

Approved, 5-1, adoption of the General Fund, Capital Fund, Wastewater Fund, Stormwater Fund, Old Town Service District Fund, Cable Grant Fund, Transit Fund, and Transportation Tax Fund Budgets for the 2018 fiscal year and the adoption of the 2018 – 2022 Capital Improvement Program and the FY2018 schedule of rates and levies. (Councilmember Schmidt opposed.)

 

Approved, unanimously, to incorporate all the changes to the budget change document and further revised following the earlier work session.

 

Approved, unanimously, to set the amount of the employee market wage adjustment (cost of living increase) for all qualified full-time classified employees and permanent part-time employees at 2 percent effective July 1, 2017.

 

Approved, unanimously, to set the amount of the employee merit raise for all qualified full-time classified employees and permanent part-time employees at 2.5 percent effective July 1, 2017.

 

Approved, unanimously, to approve the health benefit contribution rates for the City of Fairfax and eligible employees for fiscal year 2018 as specified in Attachment 2 and further move that the City Manager is directed to implement the employee benefits as provided for and described in the adopted fiscal year 2018 budget.

 

Approved, 5-1, to set the real estate tax rate for the 2017 tax year at $1.06 per one hundred dollars of assessed value, of which $.025 per one hundred dollars of assessed value shall be dedicated to the Stormwater Fund, to set the personal property and machinery and tools tax rate for the 2017 tax year at $4.13 per one hundred dollars assessed valuation based upon the assessment ratio of one hundred percent of fair market value, and to set the personal property tax rate for the 2017 tax year at $.01 per one hundred dollars assessed valuation for those members of volunteer fire departments or volunteer rescue squads qualifying under the City Code. (Councilmember Schmidt opposed.)
 
 

5.

Council Comments.

 

6.

Adjournment.
