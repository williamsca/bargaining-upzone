                     COUNCIL ACTIONS OF
                         September 9, 2014 
 
 1.  Call the Regular Meeting to order.
 
 2.  Invocation (Councilmember Loftus).
 
 3.  Pledge of Allegiance.
 
 4.  Presentations and Proclamations.

a.

Presentation of a proclamation declaring September 20, 2014 as GIRL, Inc. Day in the City of Fairfax.

 
 
 5.  Presentations by the public on any item not subject to a public hearing.
 
 6.  Adoption of the agenda.
 
 7.  Consent agenda.

a.

Introduction of an appropriation resolution in an amount not to exceed $25,000 to conduct a planning workshop ("charrette") for downtown in collaboration with the Northern Virginia Regional Commission (NVRC) and George Mason University (Mason).

 

b.

Introduction of a resolution approving an appropriation of $659,778 for synthetic turf installation at Fairfax High School.

 

c.

Consideration of a resolution approving the reimbursement of $659,778 in expenditures relating to the acquisition and installation of synthetic turf at the Fairfax High School athletic field with lease proceeds.

 

d.

Consideration of an award of contract to TischlerBise in an amount not to exceed $55,000 for a development related Fiscal/Economic Impact Model.

 

e.

Consideration of an amendment to revise a 1990 City policy regarding the sale and consumption of alcoholic beverages on City property or on property under the City’s control during specific city sponsored events.

 

f.

Consideration of the FY 2015-18 Community Services Board State Performance Contract.

 

g.

Consideration of an amendment to a resolution to expand membership of the Human Services Committee to include the City’s representative to the Fairfax County Library Board of Trustees.

 
Approved, unanimously, Consent Agenda items 7a-g. (Councilman Greenfield was absent.)
 
 
 
 8.  Public hearings. None.
 
 9.  Items not requiring a public hearing.

a.

Consideration of a resolution approving a lease-purchase agreement with US Bancorp Government Leasing & Finance, Inc. to finance the purchase of synthetic turf for Fairfax High School’s athletic field and fourteen (14) vehicles and pieces of equipment in FY2015.

 
Approved, unanimously, to adopt a resolution approving a lease-purchase agreement with US Bancorp Government Leasing & Finance, Inc. to finance the purchase of synthetic turf for Fairfax High School’s athletic field and fourteen (14) vehicles and pieces of equipment in FY2015. (Councilman Greenfield was absent.)
 
 

b.

Consideration of a resolution naming the new downtown park "Old Town Square."

 
Approved, 4-1, to adopt a resolution naming the new downtown park “Old Town Square." (In favor: Councilmembers DeMarco, Meyer,  Schmidt, Miller; opposed: Councilmember Loftus; absent: Councilman Greenfield.)
 
 
 
 10.  Approval of minutes.

a.

July 22, 2014 special meeting.

 

b.

July 22, 2014 regular meeting.

 
 
 11.  Closed meeting to discuss appointments to Boards and Commissions and for consultation from legal counsel.
 
 12.  Actions out of closed meeting/Appointments to Boards and Commissions.
  • Unanimously, reappointed Robert Kalmin to the Board of Architectural Review for a three-year term extending through September 20, 2017. (Councilman Greenfield was absent.)
  • Unanimously, reappointed Fernando Sepulveda to the Board of Architectural Review for a three-year term extending through October 25, 2017. (Councilman Greenfield was absent.)
  • Unanimously, appointed Mark Angres to the Board of Architectural Review for the remainder of a three-year term extending through September 30, 2016. (Councilman Greenfield was absent.)
  • Unanimously, reappointed Brian Knapp to the Parks and Recreation Advisory Board for a three-year term extending through September 30, 2017. (Councilman Greenfield was absent.)
  • Unanimously, appointed Sarah Nutter to the Economic Development Authority for the remainder of a four-year term extending through November 1, 2015.  (Councilman Greenfield was absent.)
  • Approved, 4-1 to direct the city staff to negotiate the terms and form of an agreement with Fairfax County Water Authority ("Fairfax Water") under which the City will agree to pay, on an accelerated basis, the amount due to Fairfax Water from the City for the City’s participation in the Fairfax Water System.  and further move that this matter be placed on the September 23, 2014 City Council meeting agenda for consideration of the agreement when finalized. (In Favor: Councilmembers DeMarco, Meyer, Miller, Schmidt; opposed: Councilmember Loftus.) (Councilman Greenfield was absent.)
  • Approved, 4-1, to introduce an appropriation resolution in the amount not to exceed $20M to satisfy the City’s obligation due to Fairfax County Water Authority pursuant to the terms of the January 3, 2014, asset purchase agreement promissory note and waive the first reading and set the public hearing for September 23, 2014. (In favor: Councilmembers DeMarco, Meyer,  Schmidt, Miller; opposed: Councilmember Loftus; absent: Councilman Greenfield.)
 
 
 
 13.  Comments by City Council.
 
 14.  Adjournment.
