6.

Consent agenda.

 

a.

Introduction of an ordinance amending Section 98-1 of the Code of the City of Fairfax, Virginia, pertaining to state motor vehicle laws.

 

b.

Consideration of a resolution for the extension of a parking lot lease agreement between the City and The Business Investment Group, LLC, for the lease of approximately 46 parking spaces located at the intersection of Sager Avenue and University Drive.

Consideration of a resolution setting the allocation percentage for Personal Property Tax Relief in the City of Fairfax for the 2014 Tax Year.

Approved, unanimously, the consent agenda.
 
 

7.

Public hearings.

 

a.

Public hearing and Council action on an appropriation resolution in the amount of $990,463 for FY 2014 nondiscretionary expenditures.

 
Approved, unanimously, an appropriation resolution in the amount of $990,463 for FY 2014 nondiscretionary expenditures.
 
 

b.

Public hearing and Council action on an appropriation resolution in the amount of $341,602 for FY 2013-2014 Federal and State Grant Expenditures.

 
Approved, unanimously, an appropriation resolution in the amount of $341,602 for FY 2013-2014 federal and state grant expenditures.
 
 

c.

Public hearing and Council action on appropriation resolutions for the General, Capital, Wastewater, Transit, Stormwater, Cable, Old Town, and Transportation Tax Funds for the fiscal year beginning July 1, 2014.

 
Approved, unanimously, the appropriation resolutions for the General, Capital, Wastewater, Transit, Stormwater, Cable, Old Town, and Transportation Tax Funds for the fiscal year beginning July 1, 2014.
 
 

d.

Public hearing and Council action on an ordinance amending certain sections of Article II, Chapter 66 of the Code of the City of Fairfax, Virginia pertaining to the supplemental retirement plan for general employees.

 
Approved, 4-3, an ordinance amending certain sections of Article II, Chapter 66 of the Code of the City of Fairfax, Virginia, pertaining to the retirement plan for general employees. (In favor, Mayor Silverthorne and Councilmembers DeMarco, Greenfield, and Stombres; opposed, Councilmembers Drummond, Meyer, and Schmidt.)
 
 

e.

Public hearing and Council action on an ordinance amending certain sections of Article III, Chapter 66 of the Code of the City of Fairfax, Virginia pertaining to the supplemental retirement plan for public safety employees.

 
Approved, 4-3, an ordinance amending certain sections of Article III, Chapter 66 of the Code of the City of Fairfax, Virginia, pertaining to the retirement plan for public safety employees. (In favor, Mayor Silverthorne and Councilmembers DeMarco, Greenfield, and Stombres; opposed, Councilmembers Drummond, Meyer, and Schmidt.)
 
 

f.

Public hearing and Council action on a Supplemental Appropriation Resolution in the amount of $1,284,247 for the transfer of surplus Water Fund monies to the Transportation Tax Fund to fully fund the Commercial and Industrial Tax at its equivalent maximum rate.

 
Approved, unanimously, a supplemental appropriation in the amount of $1,284,247 for the transfer of surplus water fund monies to the transportation tax fund to fully fund the commercial and industrial tax at its equivalent maximum rate.
 
 

g.

Public hearing and Council action on an ordinance authorizing "Quick-Take" Condemnation of a public street dedication (right of way), permanent utility easement, permanent joint use utility easement, and temporary construction easement on certain real property owned by Fairfax Crossroads Company, LLC at 10996-11002 Fairfax Boulevard, and having Tax Map No. 57-1-02-040. (In conjunction with the Kamp Washington Road Improvement Project.)

 
Approved, unanimously, an ordinance authorizing the initiation of “quick-take” condemnation proceedings for the acquisition of a street dedication (right of way) and various easements on property owned by Fairfax Crossroads Company at 10996-11002 Fairfax Boulevard, and having tax map no. 57-1-02-040.
 
 

h.

Public hearing and Council action on an ordinance authorizing "Quick-Take" Condemnation of a permanent utility easement and temporary construction easement on certain real property owned by CH Realty VI/R Fairfax Pointe, LLC at 10955 Fairfax Boulevard, and having Tax Map No. 57-1-14-001-A. (In conjunction with the Kamp Washington Road Improvement Project.).

 
Approved, unanimously, an ordinance authorizing the initiation of “quick-take” condemnation proceedings for the acquisition of various easements on property owned by CH Realty VI/R Fairfax Pointe, LLC at 10955 Fairfax boulevard, and having tax map no. 57-1-14-001-A.
 
 

i.

Public hearing and Council action on an ordinance authorizing "Quick-Take" Condemnation of permanent signal control easements and a temporary construction easement on certain real property owned by Westmore Shopping Center Associates at 10911-10927 Main Street, and having Tax Map No. 57-1-24-047 and 57-1-24-047A. (In conjunction with the Kamp Washington Road Improvement Project.).

 
Approved, unanimously, an ordinance authorizing the initiation of “quick-take” condemnation proceedings for the acquisition of various easements on property owned by Westmore Shopping Center Associates at 10911-10927 Main Street, and having tax map nos. 57-1-24-047 and 57-1-24-047A.

a.

May 13, 2014 regular meeting.

 

10.

Comments by City Council.

 
Approved, unanimously, to authorize and approve the settlement, on the terms and conditions outlined by the City Attorney in closed meeting, of Christopher P. Avery v. City of Fairfax, Virginia (Fairfax County Circuit Court No. 2013-13852), relating to an erroneous tax assessment claim and a lien for non-payment of City meals taxes of Archimedes IV, LLC d/b/a Icons Sports Lounge and Grill. Councilman Drummond further moved to authorize the City Attorney to finalize the negotiations and any settlement agreement in this matter and to authorize the City Attorney, the City Treasurer, and the City Manager, as applicable, to execute any documentation necessary to finalize the settlement and to conclude the court case. (Councilman Stombres was absent from the vote.)
 
 

11.

Appointments to Boards and Commissions.

 
Approved, unanimously, to reappoint Jeanie Bell Winslow, Brenda Kuhlman, Stephanie Yost, and Debbie Bermudez-Bernier to the Commission for Women for three-year terms extending through June 30, 2017. (Councilman Stombres was absent from the vote.)

Approved, unanimously, to appoint Bill Brown to the Board of Building Code Appeals for a five-year term extending through June10, 2019. Approved, unanimously, to appoint Mark Farage to the Fairfax Area Disability Services Board for a three-year term extending through June10, 2017.  (Councilman Stombres was absent from the vote.)

Approved, unanimously, to appoint John Norce to the Health Systems Agency for the remainder of a three-year term extending through November 1, 2014. (Councilman Stombres was absent from the vote.)  

Approved, unanimously, to appoint Suzanne Levy to the Library Board for a four-year term extending through June10, 2018. (Councilman Stombres was absent from the vote.)

Approved, unanimously, to appoint Jon Stehle to the Parks and Recreation Advisory Board for the remainder a three-year term extending through June 30, 2014, with an extension of a three-year term extending through June 30, 2017 and Michael Anderson to the Parks and Recreation Advisory Board for the remainder a three-year term extending through September 1, 2016. (Councilman Stombres was absent from the vote.)

Approved, unanimously, to appoint Tom Armstrong to the Planning Commission for the remainder a four-year term extending through December 31, 2015 and Janet Jaworski to the Planning Commission for the remainder a four-year term extending through December 31, 2017.  (Councilman Stombres was absent from the vote.)
