 7.  Public hearings.

a.

Public hearing and Council action on a request of ALDI, Inc. by Jacob DiPiazza, Director of Real Estate, pursuant to City Code Section 110-782 for a Special Use Permit to allow a grocery store greater than 5,000 sq. ft. in the C-2 Retail Commercial and Highway Corridor Overlay districts, and pursuant to City Code Section 110-185 for a Special Exception to allow tenant signage on walls other than building frontage at a shopping center, on the land known as 11001 Lee Highway and more particularly described as tax map 57-1-02-128.

 
Approved, unanimously, the request of ALDI Inc., Applicant, by Jacob DiPiazza, Director of Real Estate, for a special permit to allow a grocery store greater than 5,000 sq. ft. in the C-2 Retail Commercial District and Highway Corridor Overlay District on the premises known as 11001 Lee Highway, and more particularly described as Tax Map Parcel 57-1-02-128 subject to the following conditions: 1. Approval shall be in substantial conformance with the applicant’s submitted materials and plans dated 03-26-2014 and 02-11-2014.   Any nonconformance shall necessitate review and approval before City Council.

2. Delivery trucks and garbage trucks servicing the ALDI grocery store shall enter and leave the site via the signalized entrance onto Lee Highway (RT. 29). These same delivery and garbage trucks shall be restricted to accessing the site between the hours of 7:00 a.m. and 7:00 p.m., Monday through Friday, and 9:00 a.m. through 7:00 p.m. Saturday and Sunday.

3. ALDI-generated garbage will be picked up twice a week minimum from the store to limit the duration that food waste will remain in the on-site dumpsters.

(Councilman Stombres was absent.)
 
 

b.

Public hearing on the City Manager’s proposed FY 2014-2015 budget.

 

c.

Public hearing and Council action on an ordinance amending Chapter 90, Article II, Division 3 of the Code of the City of Fairfax, Virginia, pertaining to real property tax exemptions, deferrals and freezes for certain elderly or disabled taxpayers.

 
Approved, unanimously, that the City Council adopt an ordinance amending Chapter 90, Article II, Division 3 of the Code of the City of Fairfax, Virginia, pertaining to real property tax exemptions, deferrals and freezes for certain elderly and disabled taxpayers. (Councilman Greenfield was absent from the vote and Councilman Stombres was absent.)
 
 

d.

Public hearing and Council action on an ordinance amending Chapter 70, Article II, Section 70-36 of the Code of the City of Fairfax, Virginia, pertaining to the calculation of rental relief for elderly or disabled persons.

 
Approved, unanimously, to adopt an ordinance amending Chapter 70, Article II, section 36(a) pertaining to rental relief for elderly or disabled persons. (Councilman Stombres was absent.)
 
 

e.

Public hearing and Council action on an Ordinance authorizing the City Manager to execute an agreement with Layton Hall Limited Partnership, to install and maintain two (2) storm sewer outfalls within a portion of the City’s right-of-way for a period of five (5) years.

 
Approved, unanimously, to adopt an ordinance approving a license agreement with Layton Hall Limited Partnership, for use of a portion of the public right-of-way for installation and maintenance of two (2) storm sewer outfalls. (Councilman Stombres was absent.)
 
 

f.

Public hearing and Council action on an ordinance to affect the financing required for the development and improvement of City owned downtown properties, to include the City’s Old Town Square Park, the East Street parking lot and the North Street pedestrian barrier, and the renovation and expansion of the City’s Police Department Firearms Training Center located on Pickett Road.

 
Approved, unanimously, an ordinance calling for awarding a ground lease to affect the financing needed for the development and improvement of City owned downtown properties, to include the City’s Old Town Square Park, the East Street parking lot and the North Street pedestrian barrier, and the renovation and expansion of the City’s Police Firearms Training Center. (Councilmember Schmidt was absent from the vote and Councilman Stombres was absent.)
 
 

g.

Public hearing and Council action on an appropriation resolution in the amount not to exceed $8,100,000 for the development and improvement of City owned downtown properties, to include the City’s Old Town Square Park, the East Street parking lot and the North Street pedestrian barrier, and the renovation and expansion of the City’s Police Department Firearms Training Center located on Pickett Road.

 
Approved unanimously, an appropriation resolution in the amount of $8,100,000 for the development and improvement of City owned downtown properties, to include the City’s Old Town Square Park, the East Street parking lot and the North Street pedestrian barrier, and the renovation and expansion of the City’s Police Firearms Training Center.
