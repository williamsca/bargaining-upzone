a.

Public hearing and Council action on a requests of the Rebkee Companies, applicant, by Sarah Hall, agent, for special use permits to allow a medical care facility in the C-2 Retail Commercial District as defined by City Code Section 110-762 (a)(6) pursuant to City Code Section 110-782 (a)(1) and to allow a commercial use or development in the Highway Corridor Overlay District seeking vehicular access from a local street which also provides access to residential areas pursuant to City Code Section 110-874(b)(3); and for special exceptions to allow parking within the 25 foot building restriction area pursuant to City Code Section 110-259 (a)(1)(a) and to allow a land disturbance of greater than 2,500 square feet in a Resource Protection Area pursuant to City Code Section 110-89 (b) on the premises known as 10100 Fairfax Boulevard, more particularly described as Tax Map Parcel 47-4 03 000 C. (Continued from the October 28, 2014 City Council Meeting.)

 

Approved, unanimously, a request of Rebkee Companies, applicant, by Sarah Hall, agent, for special use permits pursuant to City Code Section 110-782 (a)(1) to:

  1. Allow for a medical care facility as defined in City Code Section 110-762(a)(6) pursuant to City Code Section 110-782(a)(1),
  2. Allow a commercial use or development in the Highway Corridor Overlay District seeking vehicular access from a local street which also provides access to residential areas pursuant to City Code Section 110-874(b)(3),

on the premises known as 10100 Fairfax Boulevard, and more particularly described as tax map parcels 47-4-23-000-c subject to the following conditions:

  1. Final plans shall be in substantial conformance with the Special Use Permit Plat dated June 18, 2014, revised October 3, 2014 and stamped by the engineer on October 14, 2014.
  2. All building facades shall incorporate a mixture of brick, precast concrete, EIFS, and other materials as shown on the attached perspective renderings, with final approval of materials, colors, banding and landscape subject to reviewal and approval by the Board of Architectural Review.
  3. All overhead utilities located on-site, as well as those which cross Plantation Parkway shall be undergrounded.
  4. The applicant shall not prohibit the general public from parking on the property in order to use the US Postal Service mail box located in the adjacent right-of-way for as long as that mail box is at that location.
  5. Within 30 days after issuance of a non-residential use permit, the applicant shall provide a contribution to the City to be determined by the Department of Parks and Recreation, not to exceed $7,000, to be used for the design, construction and installation of signage for the Snyder Trail.
  6. The applicant shall provide an easement for staging and access for the construction of the proposed Snyder Trail with an approximate area as shown in Exhibit 1. Duration of the easement shall be determined in consultation with the City, but shall not commence prior to February 1, 2015, or extend beyond June 1, 2015.
  7. Tenant signage shall be limited to one ground mounted sign and two building mounted signs, exclusive of directional signage. Building mounted signs shall be confined to the south and east faces of the building and individual sign dimensions shall not exceed dimensions as shown in the approved Special Use Permit Plat.

 

Approved, unanimously, a request of Rebkee Companies, applicant, by Sarah Hall, agent, for special exceptions pursuant to City Code Section 110-263, to:

  1. Reduce the required parking landscape strip and to allow a land disturbance of greater than 2,500 square feet in a Resource Protection Area pursuant to City Code Section 110-89,

on the premises known as 10100 Fairfax Boulevard, and more particularly described as tax map parcels 47-4-23-000-c subject to the following conditions:

  1. Final plans shall be in substantial conformance with the Special Use Permit Plat dated June 18, 2014, revised October 3, 2014 and stamped by the engineer on October 14, 2014.
  2. All building facades shall incorporate a mixture of brick, precast concrete, EIFS, and other materials as shown on the attached perspective renderings, with final approval of materials, colors, banding and landscape subject to reviewal and approval by the Board of Architectural Review.
  3. All overhead utilities located on-site, as well as those which cross Plantation Parkway shall be undergrounded.
  4. The applicant shall not prohibit the general public from parking on the property in order to use the US Postal Service mail box located in the adjacent right-of-way for as long as that mail box is at that location.
  5. Within 30 days after issuance of a non-residential use permit, the applicant shall provide a contribution to the City to be determined by the Department of Parks and Recreation, not to exceed $7,000, to be used for the design, construction and installation of signage for the Snyder Trail.
  6. The applicant shall provide an easement for staging and access for the construction of the proposed Snyder Trail with an approximate area as shown in Exhibit 1. Duration of the easement shall be determined in consultation with the City, but shall not commence prior to February 1, 2015, or extend beyond June 1, 2015.
  7. Tenant signage shall be limited to one ground mounted sign and two building mounted signs, exclusive of directional signage. Building mounted signs shall be confined to the south and east faces of the building and individual sign dimensions shall not exceed dimensions as shown in the approved Special Use Permit Plat.
 
 

b.

Public hearing and Council action on requests of The Lamb Center, applicant, by Sarah Hall, agent, for  a Special Use Permit as defined by City Code Section 110-822 (7), pursuant to Section 110-843 (1), to allow a social service delivery establishment in the I-2 Industrial District; for Special Exceptions pursuant to Section 110-263, to modify the 25-foot screening and perimeter landscaping requirements; pursuant to Section 110-845(6), to modify the 25-foot front yard setback; pursuant to Section 110-158, to modify the requirement to provide separation from a structure; and as defined by Section 110-105 (k), pursuant to Section 110-369, to allow utilities in the adjoining right-of-way to remain above ground; and for Subdivision Variances pursuant to Section 86-10, to waive the requirements for a dead-end street (cul-de-sac) and a concrete sidewalk on the premises known as 3160 and 3168 Campbell Drive, and more particularly described as Tax Map Parcels 48-3((9))28 & 29.

 

Approved, unanimously, a request of The Lamb Center, applicant, by Sarah Hall, agent, for a Special Use Permit pursuant to Section 110-843 (1) to allow a social service delivery establishment in the I-2 Industrial District and for special exceptions pursuant to Section 110-263, Section 110-845(6), Section 110-158, Section 110-105 (k), and Section 110-369, to:

  1. Reduce the 25-foot screening requirement.
  2. Reduce the perimeter landscaping requirement.
  3. Modify the 25-foot front yard setback requirement.
  4. Allow a drive-aisle without separation from the building.
  5. Allow utilities in the right-of-way to remain above ground.

on the premises known as 3160 and 3168 Campbell Drive, and more particularly described as Tax Map Parcels 48-3((9))28 & 29, subject to the following conditions:

1.   Development of the site shall be in substantial conformance with the attached Special Use Permit and Special Exception Plat dated May 15, 2014 and revised through October 27, 2014 and the Architectural Plan dated June 5, 2014 and revised through October 28, 2014.

2.   The operation of the facility shall be in substantial conformance with the attached Communications/Community Outreach plan, Loitering Plan, Litter Control Plan, Business Operation Program and Monitoring and Maintenance Plan prepared on August 29, 2014.

3.   During the first year of operation, the applicant shall meet on a quarterly basis with City staff with a report to the City Council to review calls for service and the loitering, monitoring, and maintenance plans.

4. The hours of operation for The Lamb Center shall be the following:

  • Monday through Friday- 8:00 a.m. -3:00 p.m.
  • Saturday- 8:00 a.m.-12:30 p.m.

and during the months of April through October, The Lamb Center shall be open Friday evenings from 6:00 p.m. through 8:30 p.m.

 

Approved, unanimously, a request of The Lamb Center, applicant, by Sarah Hall, agent, for subdivision variances pursuant to Section 86-10 to:

  1. Waive the requirement for a turnabout on a dead-end street,
  2. Waive the public improvement requirement to provide a concrete sidewalk,

on the premises known as 3160 and 3168 Campbell Drive, and more particularly described as Tax Map Parcels 48-3((9))28 & 29, subject to the following conditions:

1.   Development of the site shall be in substantial conformance with the attached Special Use Permit and Special Exception Plat dated May 15, 2014 and revised through October 27, 2014 and the Architectural Plan dated June 5, 2014 and revised through October 28, 2014.

2.   The operation of the facility shall be in substantial conformance with the attached Communications/Community Outreach plan, Loitering Plan, Litter Control Plan, Business Operation Program and Monitoring and Maintenance Plan prepared on August 29, 2014.

3.   During the first year of operation, the applicant shall meet on a quarterly basis with City staff with a report to the City Council to review calls for service and the loitering, monitoring, and maintenance plans.

4. The hours of operation for The Lamb Center shall be the following:

  • Monday through Friday- 8:00 a.m. -3:00 p.m.
  • Saturday- 8:00 a.m.-12:30 p.m.

and during the months of April through October, The Lamb Center shall be open Friday evenings from 6:00 p.m. through 8:30 p.m.
 
 

c.

Public hearing and Council action on a request of Forrest Higginbotham/Lowe’s Home Centers, LLC, owners and applicant, by Molly Novotny, agent, for a Special Exception to City Code Section 110-180 to allow building mounted signage to exceed the maximum allowable sign area and be located on non-building frontage walls, to reduce the required sign setback, to allow more than one ground-mounted shopping center identification sign, and to allow ground mounted shopping center identification signage to exceed the maximum height, square footage area, and to identify the names of individual tenants, in the C-2 Retail Commercial District and the Highway Corridor Overlay District on the premises known as 4080 Jermantown Road and more particularly described as Tax Map parcel 56-2-08-26B (the former K-Mart site).

 

Approved, unanimously, a request of Forrest Higginbotham/Lowe’s Home Centers, LLC, owners and applicant, by Molly Novotny, agent, for a Special Exception pursuant to Section 110-180 to:

  1. Allow building-mounted signage to exceed the ratio of 1.5 square feet for each linear foot of building frontage and the limitation of 150 cumulative square feet and be located on non-frontage walls.
  2. Allow more than 1 ground-mounted shopping center identification sign.
  3. Allow ground-mounted shopping center identification signage to exceed maximum height, area, and identify individual tenants.

on the premises known as 4080 Jermantown Road and more particularly described as Tax Map parcel 56-2-08-26B, subject to the following conditions:

1.  All signage shall be in substantial conformance with the attached Lowe’s signage graphics (pages 1 – 14), dated September 17, 2014 and revised through October 29, 2014, Sign Exhibit EXH-1 dated 10-28-2014, and the Sign Plan Matrix, dated as revised through October 29, 2014 or as further amended by these conditions.

2.  If any future public right-of-way improvements that require the removal of Sign F, then any and all costs associated with the removal, and any future relocation or modification of Sign F, shall be fully and wholly borne by the landowner or its assigns and not by the City of Fairfax or its assigns.
