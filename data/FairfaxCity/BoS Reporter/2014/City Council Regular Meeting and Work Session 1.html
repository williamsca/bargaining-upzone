                     COUNCIL ACTIONS OF
                         January 28, 2014 
 
 1.  Invocation.  (Councilman Drummond)
 
 2.  Pledge of Allegiance.
 
 3.  Presentations and Proclamations.  None.
 
 4.  Presentations by the public on any item not subject to a public hearing.
 
 5.  Adoption of the agenda.
 
 6.  Consent agenda.
All consent agenda items were removed for discussion.
 
 

a.

Consideration of a resolution authorizing the City Manager to enter into a Memorandum of Agreement with the Northern Virginia Transportation Authority (NVTA) regarding NVTA’s distribution of the 30% funds to the City of Fairfax.

 
Approved unanimously, to authorize the City Manager to enter into a Memorandum of Agreement with the Northern Virginia Transportation Authority regarding NVTA's distribution of the 30% funds to the City of Fairfax provided that the agreement and any amendments are acceptable to the City Manager and approved as to form by the City Attorney.
 
 

b.

Consideration of a resolution authorizing the City Manager to enter into a Memorandum of Agreement with the Northern Virginia Transportation Commission (NVTC) authorizing the NVTC to act on the City’s behalf as the contracting and coordinating agent for the New Electronic Payment Program (NEPP).

 
Approved, unanimously, to authorize the City Manager to enter into a Memorandum of Agreement with the Northern Virginia Transportation Commission authorizing the NVTC to act on the City's behalf as the contracting and coordinating agent for the new electronic payment program, provided that the agreement and any amendments are acceptable to the City Manager and approved as to form by the City Attorney.
 
 

c.

Consideration of a resolution to approve the City’s submission to the Northern Virginia Transportation Authority for FY 14-16 NVTA 70% funding.

 
Approved, unanimously, to adopt a resolution to approve the City's submission to the Northern Virginia Transportation Authority for FY 14-16 NVTA 70% funding.
 
 

d.

Consideration of a resolution to request a transfer of a section of public road (a portion of frontage road along Fairfax Boulevard between Pickett Road and Fairfax Circle) from the Virginia Department of Transportation to the City of Fairfax.

 
Approved, unanimously, to adopt a resolution to request that the Virginia Department of Transportation and the Commonwealth Transportation Board transfer a section of public road (road frontage along Fairfax Boulevard between Pickett Road and Fairfax Circle) to the City of Fairfax.
 
 

e.

Consideration of a resolution to authorize proceeding with the requirements to place an advisory referendum on the May 6, 2014 ballot, with respect to the question of changing the terms of office, from two years to four years, for city councilmembers and the mayor.

 
Approved, 5-1, to adopt a resolution regarding an advisory referendum to be conducted on November 4, 2014 pertaining to increasing the terms of office of city councilmembers, the mayor and the members of the school board from two years to four years. (In favor: Councilmembers DeMarco, Drummond, Greenfield, Meyer and Stombres; opposed: Councilmember Schmidt.)
 
 
 
 7.  Public hearings.

a.

Public hearing and Council action on the issuance of up to $25,000,000 in Sewer System Revenue Refunding Bonds of the City of Fairfax, Virginia, to refund the City’s outstanding local lease acquisition agreement and financing lease dated as of October 15, 2010, with the Virginia Resources Authority.

 
Approved, unanimously, a resolution approving the issuance of its Sewer System Revenue Refunding Bond, Series 2014, and setting forth the form, details and provisions for the payment thereof and approving the award of such Bond to the Virginia Resources Authority in exchange for the cancellation of prior lease obligations of the City. 
 
 

b.

Public hearing and Council action on an ordinance vacating a portion of a City waterline easement in Loudoun County, Virginia, located on lands of Section 69-Ashburn Village.

 
This item was removed from the agenda per the City Attorney.
 
 

c.

Public hearing and Council action on an appropriation resolution in the amount of $84,200 for the replacement of a natural gas line at the City property yard.

 
Approved, unanimously, an appropriation resolution in the amount of $84,200 for the replacement of a natural gas line at the City Property Yard.
 
 

d.

Public hearing and Council action on an ordinance adopting Supplement No. 15 to the Code of the City of Fairfax, Virginia.

 
Approved, unanimously, an ordinance adopting loose-leaf supplement number fifteen to the Code of the City of Fairfax, Virginia.
 
 
 
 8.  Items not requiring a public hearing.      None.

a.

January 14, 2014 regular meeting.

 
 
 10.  Comments by City Council.

 13.  Reconvene the regular meeting.
 
 14.  Closed meeting to discuss appointments to Boards and Commissions and to consult with legal counsel for legal advice.
 
 15.  Actions out of the closed meeting/appointments to Boards and Commissions.
Approved unanimously, to authorize and approve the settlement of the City of Fairfax v. Swart Northwest Corner Associates L.P. (Fairfax County Circuit Court No. CL2012-11079), relating to condemnation of property located at 11200 Fairfax Boulevard on the terms and conditions outlined by the City Attorney in closed meeting. Councilman Drummond further moved to authorize the City Attorney to finalize the negotiations and any settlement agreement in this matter and to authorize the City Attorney and the City Manager, as applicable, to execute any documentation necessary to finalize the settlement.
 
 
Confirmed Paul Cunningham as the Planning Commission Representative on the Board of Architectural Review for a one-year term extending through February 7, 2015. 

Confirm Jennifer Passey as the Planning Commission Representative on the Parks and Recreation Advisory Board for a one-year term extending through February 7, 2015.
