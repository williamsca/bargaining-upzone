Introduction of an Ordinance (i) amending Chapter 38, Article III, Section 38-31, of the Code of the City of Fairfax, Virginia, (ii) redesignating Chapter 38, Articles IV, V and VI as new Chapter 38, Articles V, VI and VII, and (iii) adding a new Chapter 38, Article IV, relating to Cross Connection Control and Backflow Prevention.

d.

Receive list of delinquent taxes from the City Treasurer in compliance with State Code Section 58.1-3921, write off taxes in compliance with State Code Section 58.1-3940 and consideration to publish the list of delinquent personal property taxes of more than $100 and delinquent real estate taxes.

Approved, unanimously, items 6 a-c.  (Councilman Greenfield was absent.) 
 
 
 
 7.  Public hearings.

a.

Public hearing and Council action on a request of Colleen Bilowus, owner, for approval of a Special Use Permit, pursuant to City Code Section 110-482 (17), to allow a home child care facility with up to 12 children maximum, in the R-2 Residential District on the premises known as 10319 Wood Road, and more particularly described as Tax Map Parcel 56-2-06-10-003.

 
Approved, unanimously, the  request of Colleen Bilowus, owner, for a special  use permit  to allow a home child  care facili1y with  up to 12 children in the  R-2 Residential District  on the premises  known as 10319 Wood Road,  and  more particularly  described as tax map parcel 56-2-06-10-003 subject to the following conditions:

1.     The applicant may care for a maximum of twelve (12) children;

2.     No more than two employees, exclusive of the applicant, shall be permitted;

3.     The garage shall be maintained for parking of two automobiles for the residential use, and the residential use shall be limited to a maximum of four vehicles on the premises.

4.     No other signage pertaining to the child care business shall be permitted on the premises;

5.     The outdoor play area shall not be used for playing prior to 8:00 am;

6.     There shall be no modifications to the exterior of the building or site that detract from the residential character of the site;

7.     All required licenses shall be maintained at all times; and

8.     The special use permit shall be valid only for the applicant, shall not be transferrable to any other person, and shall expire after 4 years. The applicant may apply for renewal prior to the expiration of the SUP. 

(Councilman Greenfield was absent.)
 
 

b.

Public hearing and Council action on a request of Saneh Sabharwal, owner, for approval of a Special Use Permit, pursuant to City Code Section 110-482 (17), to allow a home child care facility with up to 12 children maximum, in the R-2 Residential District on the premises known as 10506 Oak Place, and more particularly described as Tax Map Parcel 47-4-03-043.

4.  No other signage pertaining to the child care business shall be permitted on the premises;
5.  The outdoor play area shall not be used for playing prior to 8:00 AM;

7.  All required licenses shall be maintained at all times; and
8.  The Special Use Permit shall be valid only for the applicant, shall not be transferrable to any other person, and shall expire after 4 years. The applicant may apply for renewal prior to the expiration of the SUP.(Councilman Greenfield was absent.)
