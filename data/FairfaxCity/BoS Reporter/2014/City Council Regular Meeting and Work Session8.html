 7.  Public hearings.

a.

Public hearing and Council action on a request of Combined Properties Limited Partnership, by Lee "Randy" Kenna, applicant, for a Special Use Permit pursuant to City Code Section 110-874 (b)(7) to allow multifamily dwelling units; for a Special Use Permit pursuant to City Code Section 110-782(A)(14) to allow a grocery store greater than 5,000 square feet in area; for a Special Use Permit pursuant to City Code Section 110-874(b)(2) to allow a use or development generating an estimated average daily traffic volume in excess of 700 trips; for Special Exceptions pursuant to City Code Section 110-158 to permit parking area entranceways greater than 30 feet in width, and to reduce the number of required on-site parking spaces; for Special Exceptions pursuant to City Code Section 110-263 to permit less than 10-percent on-site tree canopy, to modify the required screening, and to modify the required surface parking area perimeter landscaping ; for Special Exceptions pursuant to City Code Section 110-783(6) to increase the maximum building height to 85 feet, to modify the required front yard and bulk plane angle down zero feet on portions of the site, to reduce the required side yard area to be less than the required 10 feet, to reduce the required 25-percent landscaped open space, to increase the maximum floor area ratio, and to increase the maximum gross floor area and lot coverage for buildings and parking structures; for Special Exceptions pursuant to City Code Section 110-185 to allow a reduction in the required setback for ground mounted signage, to allow building mounted signage to extend beyond building edges and rooflines; to allow building mounted signs to project more than one-foot from the building face to which they are attached;  to allow illuminated signage within 300 feet of a residential district to be illuminated between 10:00pm and 6:00 am, to allow multiple shopping center identification signs greater than the maximum permitted area, to allow building mounted signage to exceed 1.5 square feet per each linear foot of building frontage, to allow building mounted signage to exceed 150 square feet per tenant, to allow building mounted signage to be located on walls that are not defined as building frontage, to allow temporary signage to exceed the maximum area and expiration date, to allow ground mounted signs to list tenant names, to allow window signs to cover more than 25-percent of a window, to allow a ground mounted civic sign to exceed 24 square feet, to allow multiple rental office signage to exceed four square feet,  and to allow portable signs including sandwich board signs as temporary signage; and pursuant to City Code Section 86-10 for a Variance to allow private accessways narrower than 30 feet in width in the C-2 Retail Commercial and Highway Corridor Overlay Districts on the land known as 9444-9494 Fairfax Boulevard and 9500-9544 Fairfax Boulevard and more particularly described as tax map numbers 48-3-02-011, 48-3-06-001, and a portion of tax map number 48-3-01-036A.

 

Approved by a voice vote of 5-1 recorded as follows: In favor, Councilmembers DeMarco, Drummond, Greenfield, and Meyer, and Stombres; opposed, Councilmember Schmidt, to allow multifamily residential uses in the Highway Corridor Overlay District; pursuant to City Code Section 110-874(b)(2) to allow a use and development located in the Highway Corridor Overlay District that generates an estimated average daily traffic volume in excess of 700 trips per day; and pursuant to City Code Section 110-782(a)(14) to allow a grocery store with a floor area greater than 5,000 square feet in the C-2 Retail Commercial District.

 

Approved by a voice vote of 5-1 recorded as follows: In favor, Councilmembers DeMarco, Drummond, Greenfield, and Meyer, and Stombres; opposed, Councilmember Schmidt, to adopt a resolution contained within the staff report, which includes 23 special exceptions.

 

Approved by a voice vote of 5-1 recorded as follows: In favor, Councilmembers DeMarco, Drummond, Greenfield, and Meyer, and Stombres; opposed, Councilmember Schmidt for a Subdivision Ordinance Variance V-13040060 to allow private accessways to be less than 30 feet in width as required by City Code Section 86-7.
 
 

b.

Public hearing and Council action on a request of the City of Fairfax School Board, Owner, and Superintendent Peter J. Noonan, Agent, for a Special Use Permit pursuant to City Code Section 110-482 (2) to allow expansion of a governmental use (temporary classroom trailers) in the R-2 Residential District on the premises known as Fairfax High School located at 3501 Rebel Run and more particularly described as tax map parcel 58-1-02-003.

 
Approved, unanimously, that the City Council approve the special use permit request of the City of Fairfax School Board, owner/applicant, pursuant to City Code Section 110-482(2) to allow expansion of a governmental use (temporary classroom units) located at Fairfax high School in the R-2 Residential District and Highway Corridor Overlay District (part) on the premises known as 3501 Rebel Run and more particularly described as tax map parcel 58-1-02-003, subject to the following conditions:
1. This special use permit (SUP) shall be valid for a period of five (5) years.  Development shall remain in accordance with plans previously-submitted and prepared by Fairfax County Public Schools.
2.  Should the City find it necessary to maintain the water or sewer lines in the ground beneath the temporary classroom units, as determined by the Fairfax County Water Authority, the applicant shall be responsible for all costs associated with removal and replacement of any affected temporary classroom unit facilities.

d.

Public hearing and Council action on an ordinance amending Chapter 90, Article II, Divisions 4 and 5 of the Code of the City of Fairfax, Virginia, to provide for the elimination by January 1, 2015, of the partial real property tax exemption for certain rehabilitated, renovated or replacement residential structures, and the repeal of language pertaining the partial real property tax exemption for certain substantially rehabilitated commercial structures.

 
Approved, unanimously, to adopt an ordinance amending Chapter 90, Article II, Division 4, Section 90-109, of the Code of the City of Fairfax, Virginia, pertaining to partial real property tax exemption for certain rehabilitated, renovated or replacement residential structures, and deleting Chapter 90, Article II, Division 5 of the Code of the City of Fairfax, Virginia, pertaining to partial real property tax exemption for certain substantially rehabilitated commercial structures.
