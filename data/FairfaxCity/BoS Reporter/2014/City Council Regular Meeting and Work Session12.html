                     COUNCIL ACTIONS OF
                         July 8, 2014 
 
 1.  Invocation (Councilman Meyer).
 
 2.  Pledge of Allegiance.
 
 3.  Presentations and Proclamations.  None.
 
 4.  Presentations by the public on any item not subject to a public hearing.
 
 5.  Adoption of the agenda.
 
 6.  Consent agenda.

a.

Consideration for approval of the Northern Virginia Workforce Investment Board Area XI Consortium Agreement for the period from July 2012 through June 2014.

 
Approved, unanimously.
 
 

b.

Consideration of a Task Order to Woolpert, Inc. for the preliminary design of Fairfax Boulevard and Oak Street Stormwater Improvements.

 
Approved, 5-1, to authorize the City Manager to approve the fee proposal for a task order for Woolpert, Inc. for the preliminary design of Fairfax Boulevard and Oak Street Stormwater Improvements in an amount not to exceed $65, 925.00. (In favor: Councilmembers DeMarco, Meyer, Greenfield, Schmidt, Miller; opposed Councilmember Loftus.)
 
 
 
 7.  Public hearings.

a.

Public hearing and Council action on a zoning text amendment to amend Chapter 110, Article II, Division 7 of the Code of the City of Fairfax, Virginia, pertaining to signage regulations.

 
Approved, unanimously, with an amendment, an ordinance, amending Chapter 110, Zoning, of the Code of the City of Fairfax, Virginia, specifically Article II, Division 7, Sections 110-180, 110-181 and 110-182, to revise regulations with respect to signs permitted in the Old Town Fairfax Historic Overlay District and temporary signs permitted citywide.
 
 
 
 8.  Items not requiring a public hearing.

a.

Consideration of a revised City of Fairfax and Fairfax City School Board Cooperative Agreement for Use of City Schools.

 
Approved, unanimously, the revised cooperative agreement for use of school facilities between the City of Fairfax and the City of Fairfax School Board.
 
 
 
 9.  Approval of minutes.

a.

June 24, 2014 regular meeting.

 
Approved, unanimously, the June 24, 2014 regular meeting minutes. (Councilmembers Loftus and Miller abstained.)
 
 
 
 10.  Recess the regular meeting to go into a work session.
 
 11.  Work session.

a.

Joint discussion with the Planning Commission on the Novus Fairfax Gateway LLC redevelopment project.

 

b.

Joint discussion with the Fairfax Renaissance Housing Corporation regarding future programs and opportunities.

 

c.

Discussion of the 2014-2016 City Council term proposed resolutions adopting City Council goals, policies and rules of procedures.

 
 
 12.  Reconvene the regular meeting.
 
 13.  Closed meeting to discuss appointments to Boards and Commissions.
 
 14.  Actions out of closed meeting/Appointments to Boards and Commissions.
Approved, unanimously, the 2014 appointments to regional boards and commissions.

Approved, unanimously, the concurrence with the selection of Carl Pardiny as Police Chief effective July 9, 2014.
 
 
 
 15.  Comments by City Council.
The Council reached a consensus to have a work session scheduled in either September of October to discuss the housing mix in the City and outside the City boundary.
 
 
 
 16.  Adjournment.
