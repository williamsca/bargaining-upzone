                     COUNCIL ACTIONS OF
                         September 23, 2014 
 
 1.  Call the Regular Meeting to order.
 
 2.  Invocation (Councilman DeMarco).
 
 3.  Pledge of Allegiance.
 
 4.  Presentations and Proclamations.

a.

Presentation of a proclamation acknowledging the week of October 5-11 as Fire Prevention Week.

 

b.

Presentation of a proclamation declaring November 1, 2014 as City of Fairfax Band Day.

 
 
 5.  Presentations by the public on any item not subject to a public hearing.
 
 6.  Adoption of the agenda.
 
 7.  Consent agenda.

a.

Introduction of a supplemental appropriation for FY15 30% NVTA money for the Old Town traffic study ($14,841) project.

 

b.

Consideration of a resolution authorizing the City Manager to sign the Standard Project Agreement with Northern Virginia Transportation Authority (NVTA) for FY14 NVTA 70% funding.

 

c.

Consideration of a resolution to cancel the Roadway Reconstruction Project (Route 50 Draper Drive to Fairfax Circle), designate the Kamp Washington Project as a Revenue Sharing Project and reallocate the Revenue Sharing Funds from the Roadway Reconstruction Project (VDOT UPC 104232) to the Kamp Washington Project (VDOT UPC 71614).

 
Approved, unanimously, the consent agenda.
 
 
 
 8.  Public hearings.

*This Item Is Deferred to the October 14, 2014 Regular City Council Meeting.*
a. Public hearing and Council action on a requests of Pickett LLC, owner and applicant, by Molly Novotny, agent, for a special exception pursuant to City Code Section 110-185 to allow building mounted signage in a shopping center to exceed the maximum allowable sign area and be located on non-building frontage walls; and to allow a ground-mounted shopping center identification sign to exceed the maximum height and square footage and identify the names of individual tenants, in the C-2 Retail Commercial District on the premises known as 9400-9490 Main Street as more particularly described as tax map parcel 58-3-02-029 (Pickett Shopping Center, at the northeast corner of Pickett Road and Main Street).

 

b.

Public hearing and Council action on an appropriation resolution in an amount not to exceed $25,000 to conduct a planning workshop ("charrette") for downtown in collaboration with the Northern Virginia Regional Commission (NVRC) and George Mason University (Mason).

 
Approved, 5-1, an appropriation resolution in the amount of $25,000 to conduct a planning workshop ("Charrette") for downtown in collaboration with the Northern Virginia Regional Commission (NVRC) and George Mason University (MASON). (In favor; Councilmembers DeMarco, Greenfield, Meyer, Miller, Schmidt: opposed Councilmember Loftus.)
 
 

c.

Public hearing and Council action on an appropriation resolution in the amount of $659,778 for synthetic turf installation at Stalnaker Field, Fairfax High School.

 
Approved, unanimously, an appropriation resolution for the Capital Fund for the fiscal year beginning July 1, 2014.
 
 

d.

Public hearing and Council action on an agreement with Fairfax County Water Authority under which the City will agree to pay, on an accelerated basis, the amount due to Fairfax Water from the City for the City’s participation in the Fairfax Water System.

 
Approved, 5-1, to adopt a resolution authorizing the City Manager to negotiate and execute a payment agreement in an amount not to exceed $19,000,000 to satisfy the City’s obligation due to Fairfax County Water Authority pursuant to the terms of the January 3, 2014 Asset Purchase Agreement and Promissory Note, in a form approved by the City Attorney. (In Favor, Councilmembers DeMarco, Greenfield, Meyer, Miller, and Schmidt; opposed: Councilmember Loftus.) 
 
 

e.

Public hearing and Council action on an appropriation resolution in the amount not to exceed $20M to satisfy the City’s obligation due to Fairfax County Water Authority pursuant to the terms of the January 3, 2014, asset purchase agreement promissory note.

 
Approved, 5-1, an appropriation resolution in the amount not to exceed $19,000,000 to satisfy the City’s Obligation due to Fairfax County Water Authority pursuant to the terms of the January 3, 2014 Asset Purchase Agreement and Promissory Note.  (In favor: Councilmembers DeMarco, Greenfield, Meyer, Miller and Schmidt; opposed: Councilmember Loftus.)
 
 
 
 9.  Items not requiring a public hearing.  None.
