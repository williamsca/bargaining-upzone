b.

Public hearing and Council action on a requests of ARG II, LLC, applicant, by John Napolitano/The Lann Companies, agent, for  a special use permit pursuant to Section 110-874(b)(8), to allow a temporary vehicle storage facility in the Highway Corridor Overlay District and for a special use permit pursuant to City Code Section 110-874(b)(3) to allow a commercial use in the Highway Corridor Overlay District seeking vehicular access from a local street which also provides access to residential areas on the premises known as 3580-3590 Chain Bridge Road and 10505-10511 Orchard Street, and more particularly described as Tax Map Parcels 57-2-08-11, 12 and 13 and 57-2-02-17, 18 and 19.

 

Approved, unanimously, the request of ARG II, LLC, applicant, by John Napolitano/The Lann Companies, agent, for a temporary use in the Highway Corridor Overlay District pursuant to City Code Section 110-874(b)(8), and to allow a commercial use or development in the Highway Corridor Overlay District seeking vehicular access from a local street which also provides access to residential areas pursuant to City Code Section 110-874(b)(3), on the premises known as 3580 and 3590 Chain Bridge Road and 10505, 10507, 10509 and 10511 Orchard Street, and more particularly described as tax map parcels 57-2-08-11, 12 and 13 and 57-2-02-17, 18 and 19, subject to the following conditions:

  1. The existing parking lot shall not be expanded or substantially altered in any form, except as permitted per the Special Use Permit Plat and Statement of Support received by the City of Fairfax on February 2, 2015.
  2. Parking areas must be screened with board on board fencing from Chain Bridge Road at all times. Existing board on board fencing may be relocated based on any adjustments to the edge of the parking lots and introduction of any temporary or permanent easements related to the Northfax Transportation Improvements project.
  3. If the parking area is still operating at the time that the Northfax Transportation Improvements project is designated as “substantially complete”, landscaping shall be installed to supplement the board on board fence on the property and outside any permanent easements. Landscaping shall be consistent with that approved in 2004 for the initial use of this site for a temporary use.
  4. The applicant shall ensure that use of the subject site is restricted to vehicle storage and that no activities related to the maintenance or retail sales of vehicles are conducted on the site.
  5. Vehicle transportation carriers shall be prohibited on Orchard Street.
  6. The applicant shall continue to have all existing and future lessees of the subject site sign the hold harmless agreement previously approved by the City.
  7. All tenants and all future lessees shall be notified in writing that vehicular storage lots shall be parked so as to allow fire truck access within 150’-0” within any point on each individual lot.

(Councilman DeMarco was absent.)
 
 

c.

  1. All improvements shall be in substantial conformance with the Special Use Permit Plat and Statement of Support received by the City of Fairfax on February 2, 2015.
  2. The applicant shall ensure that use of the subject site is restricted to vehicle storage and that no activities related to the maintenance or retail sales of vehicles are conducted on the site.
  3. Vehicle transportation carriers shall be prohibited on Orchard Street.
  4. All tenants and all future lessees shall be notified in writing that vehicular storage lots shall be parked so as to allow fire truck access within 150’-0” of any point on each individual lot.

(Councilman DeMarco was absent.)

 13.  Comments by City Council.

Councilmember Miller stated that a citizen recently requested an extensive amount of information regarding the Northfax Project. She thanked staff for their prompt responses to those requests and asked how much time had been spent compiling all the requested information and the total cost for those requests. She requested that information be distributed to the City Council once completed.

Councilmember Schmidt stated the City’s agreement with Verizon allows for certain areas of the City to not be able to receive FIOS and stated that the actual locations for those areas have been difficult to identify. She requested that staff contact Verizon to obtain those locations and distribute them to the City Council.

The Mayor stated that he disclosed earlier his campaign contributions from Mr. Napolitano and was alerted that his disclosure differed from what was on the affidavit. He stated that he decided to change his previous disclosure to that which was outlined in the affidavit. The Mayor stated there is some uncertainty as to when verbal disclosures are necessary and stated that there is new state legislation in regards to ethics reform. The Mayor requested that staff schedule a future work session to inform the Council on the legal requirements for disclosures and gifts.

Councilman Greenfield stated that there is a new feature on the City’s TGIFFX app that helps identify and promote downtown parking. He stated that the new app includes directions to the parking lots and the hours of operation for those lots. He requested that the new feature be publicized on the City’s website, on the Cityscreen-12, and in the Cityscene.
