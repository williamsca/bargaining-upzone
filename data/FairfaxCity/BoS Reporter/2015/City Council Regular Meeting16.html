Public hearing and Council action on a request of Novus Fairfax Gateway LLC, by Lynne J. Strobel, Agent and Attorney-in Fact, for a rezoning of the 8.33-acre site from C-2 Retail Commercial, I-2 Industrial, and Highway Corridor Overlay District, to C-2 Retail Commercial with proffers and Highway Corridor Overlay District; for a Special Use Permit pursuant to City Code Section 110-874 (b)(7) to allow multifamily dwelling units;  for a Special Use Permit pursuant to City Code Section 110-874(b)(2) to allow a use or development generating an estimated average daily traffic volume in excess of 700 trips; for Special Exceptions pursuant to City Code Section 110-158 to reduce the amount of required on-site parking, to reduce the required number of loading spaces, to reduce the required separation between drive aisles and parking to buildings, and to allow parking area entranceways  greater than 30 feet in width; for Special Exceptions pursuant to City Code Section 110-783(6) to increase the maximum gross floor area and lot coverage for buildings and parking structures, to increase the maximum building height to more than 5 stories or 60 feet; for Special Exceptions pursuant to City Code Section 110-263 to modify the required perimeter parking lot landscaping and screening for above grade mechanical and utilities; and for Variances to the Subdivision Ordinance pursuant to Section 86-10 to allow private accessways to be less than 24 feet in width, to reduce the minimum centerline curve radii of private accessways, and to modify the minimum requirements for dead end private accessways in the C-2 Retail Commercial, I-2 Industrial, and Highway Corridor Overlay Districts on the land known as 11101 and 11091 Fairfax Boulevard and 11160 Lee Highway 57-1-2-27, 31, 31A, & 31B.

 
Approved, unanimously, to defer the public hearing and Council action on the request of Novus Fairfax Gateway LLC to February 24, 2015. (Councilmembers DeMarco and Miller were absent.)
 
 

b.

Public hearing and Council action on a request by Fairfax Main St., LLC, by Al-Husain Y. Al-Hussein, Applicant, pursuant to City Code Section 110-1046(6) for Special Exceptions to allow development at a floor area ratio and lot coverage higher than permitted and to exceed the 43-foot maximum wall height in the C-2 Retail Commercial District and Old Town Fairfax Transition Overlay District on the premises known as 10341 Main Street and more particularly described as Tax Map Parcel 57-4-((02))-131, (Mayfair Development/Former Shell Gas Station).

 
Approved, 3-1, a resolution to approve one new condition and reaffirm 13 previously approved conditions and approve the special exception requests of Fairfax Main Street, L.L.C., owner, and Al-Husain Y. Al-Hussein, applicant, to: 1. Allow development at a floor ratio of up to 1.49 that is higher than the floor area ratio of 1.20 that is the maximum permitted and develop at a maximum lot coverage of up to 89.1 percent where city code allows 70 percent; and 2. Exceed the maximum wall height of 43 feet by providing one unit with a wall up to 45.5-feet high, for redevelopment of the property located in the C-2 Retail Commercial and Old Town Fairfax Transition Districts at 10341Main Street and more particularly described as tax map parcel 57-4-02-131, subject to the following conditions:
  1. Development shall be in general accordance with the character and quality described in the Applicant’s Statement of Support submitted with the application and with the plans including Conceptual Development Plans (CDP) by J2 Engineers (sheets 1 through 6) and by KGD (rendered elevations dated January 9, 2015) as may be revised by these conditions and by the Board of Architectural Review;
  2. All building siding and retaining walls visible from any public right-of-way shall be of brick, stone, cementitious materials supplemented with cementitious trim and detail features;
  3. The Applicant shall provide all plantings to City standards and shall provide irrigation for all plantings illustrated on the CDP;
  4. The Applicant shall either provide to City Standards and maintain on Providence Square property the plantings shown on the CDP or provide funding for Providence Square to install and maintain plants of their own choice on the west side of the Providence Square property; in either case BAR approval is required;
  5. The Applicant shall meet all standards of the City of Fairfax and of the Commonwealth of Virginia regarding both storm water detention and Chesapeake Bay water quality best management practices;
  6. Subsequent to approval of the Special Exception requests but prior to site plan approval the Applicant shall submit a construction management plan for approval by the City Manager or designee to address the following information:
              a. Hours of construction;
              b. Truck routes to and from entrances;
              c. Location of parking areas for construction employees;
              d. Truck staging and cleaning areas;
              e. Storage areas;
              f. Fencing details;
              g. Trailer and sanitary facility locations;
              h. Traffic control measures; and
              I. Maintenance of entrances;
  1. The Applicant shall provide cash, bond or letter of credit in the amount of $20,000 to provide for any damage to the road system fronting the property;
  2. The Applicant shall identify a person who shall serve as liaison to the community throughout the duration of construction. The name and telephone number of this individual shall be provided in writing to residents and business owners whose property lies adjacent to or across the street from the site.
  3. The Applicant shall provide an easement for public access over all areas where the CDP depicts sidewalk widening onto private property;
  4. The Applicant shall provide to City standards brick sidewalks, street lights and street trees along all street frontages of the property and provide additional landscaping within the rights-of-way, including shrubs, flower beds and ground cover in the triangular area of widened right-of-way adjacent to East Street;
  5. The Applicant shall be responsible for maintenance of any water or sewer mains located within or under the parking garage;
  6. All buildings shall be provided with fire suppressant systems meeting the standard of NFPA 13R at a minimum, including coverage of any attic space;
  7. The Condominium Association documents shall provide assurance that:
         a. All private garages will always remain available for parking the number of vehicles shown on the plans
         b. All decks and private courtyards visible from any public right-of-way will be kept free of toys, bicycles, laundry and other items that would give a cluttered appearance as seen from the right-of-way; and
         c. Each unit will be used only for a residential dwelling with associated accessory and/or ancillary uses.  14. The Applicant shall place underground all overhead utilities on the site and in the public rights-of-way adjacent to the site. Should estimates of the cost of placing underground those utilities adjacent to East Street exceed $250,000, the Applicant shall work with the City to achieve the underground construction at a cost to the Applicant not to exceed $250,000. As an alternative, should the City desire to do the work itself, the Applicant shall provide a one-time contribution of $250,000 to be used for undergrounding utilities around the site. If the City does not use the monies to underground the utilities within three (3) years, the money will be refunded to the Applicant.  15. To the extent permitted by State and Federal Fair Housing regulations, occupancy of each dwelling unit in the development shall be limited to no more than two (2) persons per bedroom. Questions concerning occupancy shall be governed by the terms of this condition.
(In favor, Councilmembers Greenfield, Meyer, and Schmidt; opposed, Councilmember Loftus; absent, Councilmembers DeMarco and Miller.)
