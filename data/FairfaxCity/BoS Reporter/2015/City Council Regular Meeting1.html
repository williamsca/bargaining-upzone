City of Fairfax, Virginia 22030-3630

                     COUNCIL ACTIONS OF
                         November 10, 2015 
 
 1.  Call the Regular Meeting to order.
 
 2.  Invocation (Councilmember Schmidt).
 
 3.  Pledge of Allegiance.
 
 4.  Presentations and Proclamations.

a.

Presentation of a proclamation honoring Mr. James Miller’s service to the Boy Scouts of America.

 
 
 5.  Presentations by the public on any item not subject to a public hearing.
 
 6.  Adoption of the agenda.
 
 7.  Consent agenda.

a.

Consideration of a resolution for the City’s submission to VDOT for FY2017 Revenue Sharing funding.

 
Approved, 5-1, a resolution approving the City’s submission to the Virginia Department of Transportation for FY17 Revenue Sharing funding requests. (In favor: Councilmembers Miller, Greenfield, Meyer, Schmidt, DeMarco; opposed: Councilmember Loftus.)
 
 

b.

Consideration of a resolution for the City’s submission to NVTA for FY2017-2022 RSTP/CMAQ funding.

 

c.

Consideration of a modification to an existing contract to American Mechanical Services in the amount of $155,931.00 for HVAC replacement and related mechanical/electrical work at the Blenheim house.

 
Approved, unanimously, the consent agenda items 7b-c.
 
 
 
 8.  Public hearings.

a.

Public hearing and Council action on a draft Solid Waste Management Plan for the City of Fairfax.  The purpose of the plan is to outline how the City will manage solid waste and recycling, generated by businesses and residents in the City of Fairfax, over the next twenty years.

 

Approved, unanimously, to adopt the Solid Waste Management Plan for the City of Fairfax.
 
 
 
 9.  Items not requiring a public hearing.

a.

Consideration of the City of Fairfax 2016 proposed Legislative Program.

 
Approved, unanimously, the City of Fairfax 2016 proposed Legislative Program.
 
 
 
 10.  Approval of minutes.

a.

October 27, 2015 Regular Meeting.

 
 
 11.  Closed meeting for consultation with legal counsel for legal advice and for appointments to Boards and Commissions.
 
 12.  Actions out of closed meeting/Appointments to Boards and Commissions.
  • Reappointed John Laughton to the Board of Architectural Review for a three-year term extending through September 30, 2018.
  • Reappointed Carol Caputo and Mary Lechter to the Commission on the Arts for three-year terms extending through December 31, 2018.
  • Reappointed W. Craig Havenner, Sarah Nutter, and John Sabo to the Economic Development Authority for four-year terms extending through November 1, 2019.
  • Reappointed Judy Fraser and Bruce Knight to the Environmental Sustainability Committee for two-year terms extending through November 17, 2017.
  • Reappointed Jane Albro to the Parks and Recreation Advisory Board for a three-year term extending through November 13, 2018.
 
 
 
 13.  Comments by City Council.
 
 14.  Adjournment.
