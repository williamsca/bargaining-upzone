                     COUNCIL ACTIONS OF
                         March 10, 2015 
 
 1.  Call the Regular Meeting to order.
 
 2.  Invocation (Councilman Meyer).
 
 3.  Pledge of Allegiance.
 
 4.  Presentations and Proclamations.

a.

Presentation of a contribution to the All Night Grad Party Committee of Fairfax High School by the City Council.

 

b.

Presentation of the Commercial Appearance Awards by Kirk Holley, Chair of the Community Appearance Committee.

 
 
 5.  Presentations by the public on any item not subject to a public hearing.
 
 6.  Adoption of the agenda.
 
 7.  Consent agenda.

a.

Consideration of a special event application request by Fairfax Little League for their Annual Opening Day Ceremony/Parade.

 

b.

Consideration of an award of contract to Sagres Construction Corp. in an amount not to exceed $167,551 to construct a section of the George T. Snyder Trail.

 
Approved, unanimously, the consent agenda. (Councilman DeMarco was absent.
 
 
 
 8.  Public hearings.

a.

Public hearing on City of Fairfax proposed budget for fiscal year 2015-16.

 
No action was taken.
 
 

b.

Public hearing and Council action on a zoning text amendment amending Chapter 110 Article II, Division 11, of the Code of the City of Fairfax, Virginia, pertaining to storm water management.

 
Approved, unanimously, to enact an ordinance amending Chapter 110, Article II, Division 11, Section 110-288, of the Code of the City of Fairfax, Virginia, pertaining to storm water management.  (Councilman DeMarco Was absent.)
 
 

c.

Public hearing and Council action on a zoning text amendment amending Chapter 110 Article II, Division 2, of the Code of the City of Fairfax, Virginia, pertaining to the floodplain.

 
Approved, unanimously, an ordinance repealing and reenacting Chapter 110, Article II, Division 2, of the Code of the City of Fairfax, Virginia, pertaining to flood plains and flood plain related matters.  (Councilman DeMarco was absent.)
 
 

d.

Public hearing and Council action on a Supplemental Appropriation Resolution in the amount of $60,000 for the transfer of NVTA 30% monies from the Transportation Tax Fund to the Capital Fund to fund the George T. Snyder Trail project.

 
Approved, unanimously, a supplemental appropriation in the amount of $60,000 for the transfer of NVTA 30% monies from the transportation tax fund to the capital fund to fund the George T. Snyder trail project.  (Councilman DeMarco was absent.)
 
 

e.

Public hearing and Council action on a Supplemental Appropriation Resolution in the amount of $34,976 for the transfer of NVTA 30% monies from the Transportation Tax Fund to the Downtown Fund to fund design for Old Town Sidewalk Spot Widening.

 
Approved, unanimously, a supplemental appropriation in the amount of $34,976 for the transfer of NVTA 30% monies from the transportation tax fund to the downtown fund to fund the old town sidewalk spot widening project.  (Councilman DeMarco was absent.)
 
 

f.

Public hearing and Council action on a request of Cellco Partnership d/b/a Verizon Wireless, applicant, by Ryan Dear, agent, for  a Special Use Permit pursuant to City Code Section 110-782 (a) (1) to allow a telecommunications facility that exceeds 65 feet in height when mounted on top of the commercial building in C-2 Retail Commercial District and Old Town Fairfax Transition Overlay District, on the premises known as 10555 Main Street, and more particularly described as Tax Map Parcel 57-4-02-069.
*The Applicant is requesting deferral to May 12, 2015*

 
Approved, unanimously, to defer to May 12, 2015, the public hearing and Council action on the request by Cellco Partnership d/b/a Verizon Wireless for a special use permit to allow a telecommunications facility that exceeds 65 feet in height when mounted on top of the commercial building in the C-2 Commercial District and Old Town Fairfax Transition Overlay District on the premises known as 10555 Main Street.  (Councilman DeMarco was absent.)
 
 
 
 9.  Items not requiring a public hearing.

a.

Consideration of the maximum real estate tax rate for fiscal year 2015-16 to be advertised. (This action sets the maximum real estate tax rate that can be adopted as part of the fiscal year 2015-16 budget. This action does not set the actual real estate tax rate for fiscal year 2015-16.).

 
Approved, 3-2, that the City Council advertise a proposed 2015 real estate tax rate of $1.065 per $100 of assessed value.  In favor, Councilmembers Greenfield, Meyer, and Miller; opposed, Councilmembers Loftus and Schmidt; (Councilman DeMarco was absent.)
 
 
 
 10.  Approval of minutes.

a.

February 24, 2015 Regular Meeting.

 
Approved, unanimously, the regular meeting minutes of February 24, 2015.  (Councilman DeMarco was absent.)
 
 
 
 11.  Closed meeting for appointments to Boards and Commissions.
 
 12.  Actions out of Closed Meeting/Appointments to Boards and Commissions.
Approved, unanimously, to reappoint Gail Wade to the George Mason University Fairfax Campus Advisory Board for a two-year term extending through January 11, 2017. (Councilman DeMarco was absent from the vote.)

Approved, unanimously, to appoint Eric Mittnight to the Parks and Recreation Advisory Board for the remainder of a of a three-year term extending through September 1, 2015, with an extension of a three-year term extending through September 1, 2018.  (Councilman DeMarco was absent.)
