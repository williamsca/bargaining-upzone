 8.  Public hearings.

a.

Public hearing and Council action on Request of Robinson & Thayer, Inc., by Sarah E. Hall, Attorney/Agent, for a rezoning of 3.471 acres from R-1 Residential to P-D Planned Development District with proffers; for a Waiver pursuant to City Code Section 110-634 to waive the requirement to include a commercial component of uses set forth; and for Variances to the Subdivision Ordinance pursuant to Section 86-10 to not provide curb and gutter, to reduce the private pavement surface width of less than 30 feet, to permit the ’Y’ type turnaround in lieu of a cul-de-sac, and to waive public improvements along the site’s existing streets (Chain Bridge Road and Norman Avenue), and proposed internal private street, on the land parcels known as 3500 and 3504 Chain Bridge Road and more particularly described as tax maps 47-4-15-011A and 47-4-04-015B (Cobb’s Grove).

 

Approved, unanimously, an ordinance to approve the application of Robinson and Thayer, Inc., by Sarah E. Hall, Attorney/agent, to rezone the subject property from R-1, Residential to P-D(p), Planned Development with proffers including a zoning waiver to the minimum 200 square feet of commercial gross floor area for every dwelling unit, subject to the general development plan/preliminary site plan revised through May 18, 2015 and subject to the proffers dated July 27, 2015, on the premises known as 3500 and 3504 Chain Bridge Road and more particularly described as tax map parcels 47-4-15-011a and 47-4-04-015b. The Fairfax City Council also approved and authorized the City Manager to execute, an agreement between Robinson and Thayer, Inc. and the City relating to the tendering by the applicant, and use by the City, of certain proffered funds designated for storm water and drainage improvements. (Councilmember Loftus was absent.)

Approved, unanimously, the request of Robinson and Thayer, Inc., by Sarah E. Hall, Attorney/agent, for variances to the subdivision ordinance pursuant to Section 86-10 to allow private accessways to be less than thirty (30) feet in width; to allow a dead-end street to end in a hammerhead design where a cul-de-sac is required; to allow no curb and gutter where all streets are required to have curb and gutter; and to allow private accessways and public streets without sidewalks, on the premises knows 3500 and 3504 Chain Bridge Road and more particularly described as tax map parcels 47-4-15-011a and 47-4-04-015b subject to the following conditions:

  1. Development of the Property shall be in substantial conformance with the General Development Plan/Preliminary Site Plan (“GDP/PSP”) entitled “General Development Plan/Preliminary Site Plan” prepared by Tri-Tek Engineering dated December 20, 2013, revised through May 18, 2015.
(Councilmember Loftus was absent.)
 
 

b.

Public hearing and Council action on a Supplemental Appropriation Resolution in the amount of $1,556,385 to complete the funding for the Kamp Washington Intersection Improvements Project (VDOT UPC 71614).

 
Approved, unanimously, a supplemental appropriation in the amount of $1,556,385 for the transfer of NVTA 30% monies from the Transportation Tax Fund to the Capital Fund to fund construction of the Kamp Washington Intersection Improvements Project. (Councilmember Loftus was absent.)
 
 

c.

Public hearing and Council action on a Supplemental Appropriation Resolution in the amount of $200,000 for as needed transportation planning and engineering tasks consistent with the Northern Virginia Transportation Authority’s 30% funding guidelines.

 

Approved, unanimously, a supplemental appropriation in the amount of $200,000 for the transfer of NVTA 30% monies from the Transportation Tax Fund to the Capital and Old Town Funds to fund transportation planning and engineering tasks consistent with the guidelines for 30% NVTA funding. (Councilmember Loftus was absent.)
 
 

d.

Public hearing and Council action on an ordinance amending Chapter 14, Article III, Section 14-134 and 14-136.1 of the Code of the City of Fairfax, Virginia, pertaining to home child care facility standards to reflect current health and safety regulations and update the state home child care regulations.

 

Approved, unanimously, an ordinance amending Chapter 14, Article III, Section 14-134 and 14-136.1 of the Code of the City of Fairfax, Virginia, pertaining to home child care facility standards. (Councilmember Loftus was absent.)
 
 

e.

Public hearing and Council action on an ordinance amending Chapter 2, Article II, Section 2-31, of the Code of the City of Fairfax, Virginia, to increase the salaries for the Mayor and the Members of the City Council to $13,000 and $12,000 respectively beginning with the City Council 2016-2018 term.

 
Approved, 5-1, an ordinance amending Chapter 2, Article II, Section 2-31, of the Code of the City of Fairfax, Virginia, pertaining to the salaries for the Mayor and the Members of the City Council. (Councilmember Schmidt opposed and Councilmember Loftus was absent.)
 
 

f.

Public hearing and Council action on a resolution for establishment of the City of Fairfax position regarding housing affordability.

 

Approved, unanimously and with amendments, a resolution for establishment of the City of Fairfax position regarding housing affordability. (Councilmember Loftus was absent.)
 
 

g.

Public hearing and Council action on the University Drive Road Diet one-year pilot program in an effort to improve multimodal connectivity between George Mason University and Old Town Fairfax.

 

Approved, unanimously, to direct staff to consider changes to the proposed project consistent with the comments received from the City Council and the public, finalize the plans and implement the one year pilot project.(Councilmembers Loftus and Meyer were absent.)
 
 

h.

Public hearing and Council action to seek input from the public on the proposed Roberts Road sidewalk project.

 
Approved, unanimously, to direct staff to consider changes to the proposed project consistent with the comments received from the City Council and the public, finalize the plans and proceed to right of way acquisition and construction.(Councilmembers Loftus and Meyer were absent.)
 
 
 
 9.  Items not requiring a public hearing.

a.

Consideration of a modification to the existing signal contract with VDOT to remove the Blake Lane/Lee Highway intersection and direct city manager to sign a revised agreement to that effect.

 
Approved, unanimously, to authorize the City Manager to negotiate and execute an agreement between the City and the Virginia Department of Transportation to remove the Blake Lane/Lee Highway intersection from the inventory of VDOT-owned, City-controlled intersections, provided that the new agreement and any amendments are acceptable to the City Manager and approved as to form by the City Attorney.(Councilmembers Loftus and Meyer were absent.)
 
 

b.

Consideration of an award of construction contract to Ardent Company in an estimated amount of $5,215,200  for the Kamp Washington Intersection Improvements.

 

Approved, unanimously, to award a contract to Ardent Company for an estimated amount of $5,215,200 for the Kamp Washington intersection improvements project. (Councilmembers Loftus and Meyer were absent.)
 
 

c.

Consideration of an award of construction management contract to Volkert Inc. in an estimated amount of $751,145.65 for the Kamp Washington Intersection Improvements.

 

Approved, unanimously, to award a construction management contract to Volkert, Inc. for an estimated amount of $751,145.65 for the Kamp Washington intersection improvements project. (Councilmembers Loftus and Meyer were absent.)
