a.

Public hearing on the City Manager’s Proposed FY2015-2016 Budget.

 
No action required.
 
 

b.

Public hearing and Council action on a request of Nuria Ibrahim, owner, for approval of a special use permit, pursuant to City Code Section 110-512 (18), to allow a home child care facility with up to 12 children, in the R-3 Residential District on the premises known as 10625 West Drive, and more particularly described as Tax Map Parcel 57-3-02-018.

 
Approved, unanimously,

Approved, unanimously, the request of Nuria Ibrahim, owner, for a special use permit to allow a home child care facility with up to 12 children in the R-3 Residential District on the premises known as 10625 West Drive, and more particularly described as tax map parcel 57-3-02-018 subject to the following conditions:

  1. The applicant may care for a maximum of twelve (12) children;
  2. The garage shall be maintained for parking of two automobiles for the residential use;
  3. There shall be no drop-off or parking permitted on the 20-foot outlot road/alley.
  4. There shall be no modifications to the exterior of the building or site that detract from the residential character of the site;
  5. All required licenses shall be obtained prior to start of business and maintained at all times; and
   6.  The Special Use Permit shall be valid only for the applicant, shall not be transferrable to any other person, and shall expire after 4 years. The applicant may apply for renewal prior to the expiration of the SUP.
 
 

c.

Public hearing and Council action on a request of North Street Associates, L.C., applicant, by Arthur E. Foster, agent, for a special exception, pursuant to Section 110-185, to allow tenant signage greater than three and one half feet above the finished floor level of an individual multi-tenant commercial building (Foster Building) on a single lot in the C-2 Retail Commercial and Old Town Fairfax Transition District on the premises known as 3975 University Drive, and more particularly described as Tax Map Parcel 57-2-20-002.

 

Approved, unanimously, a resolution to approve the request by North Street Associates, L.C., for a special exception pursuant to City Code Section 110-185 to allow tenant signage greater than 3.5 feet above the finished floor level of the second story of a multi-tenant commercial building on the premises known as 3975 University Drive and more particularly described as tax map 57-2-20-002, subject to the following conditions:

  1. Signage shall be in general conformance with the Sign Exhibit prepared by Art Display Company on January 13, 2015 and revised through April 21, 2015 and Sign Location Plan received February 10, 2015.
    2. The applicant shall submit an updated Master Sign Plan to the Board of Architectural Review for review prior to the granting of permits.
 
 

d.

Public hearing and Council action on an ordinance amending and readopting Chapter 90, Article II, Division 1, Section 90-33 of the Code of the City of Fairfax, Virginia, pertaining to classification and taxation of certain real property in the City of Fairfax used for or zoned for commercial and industrial purposes, and to establish a separate tax rate on such real property effective as of January 1, 2015, revenues from which would be used exclusively for transportation purposes that benefit the City of Fairfax.

 
Approved unanimously, an ordinance amending and readopting Chapter 90, Article II, Division 1, Section 90-33 of the Code of the City of Fairfax, Virginia, pertaining to classification and taxation of certain real property in the City of Fairfax used for or zoned for commercial and industrial purposes. Such ordinance shall include a tax rate for such real property for calendar year 2015 at $0.075 per $100 of assessed value, revenues from which to be used exclusively for transportation purposes that benefit the City of Fairfax.  (in favor, Councilmembers DeMarco, Greenfield, Meyer, Miller, and Schmidt; opposed, Councilmember Loftus.)
 
 

e.

Public hearing and Council action on an ordinance amending Chapter 102, Article II, Sections 102-31(b) of the Code of the City of Fairfax, Virginia to increase wastewater rates by 10.0%

 
Approved, unanimously, an ordinance amending Chapter 102, Article II, Sections 102-31 of the code of the City of Fairfax, Virginia to increase the wastewater user rates by 10.0% effective for bills rendered on or after July 1, 2015.
 
 
 
 9.  Items not requiring a public hearing.

a.

Consideration of the City Manager’s Proposed FY 2015-2016 Budget including the Rates and Levies.

 

Approved, 5-1, adoption of the General Fund, Capital Fund, Wastewater Fund, Stormwater Fund, Old Town Service District Fund, Cable Grant Fund, Transit Fund, and Transportation Tax Fund Budgets for the 2016 fiscal year and the adoption of the 2016 – 2020 Capital Improvement Program to incorporate all the changes to the budget change sheet. (In favor, Councilmembers DeMarco, Greenfield, Meyer, Miller, and Schmidt; opposed, Councilmember Loftus.)

 

Approved, unanimously, to set the amount of the employee market wage adjustment (cost of living increase) for all full-time classified employees, permanent part-time employees, City Manager, Treasurer, Commissioner of Revenue and the Registrar at 1 percent effective July 1, 2015.

 

Approved, 5-1, to set the amount of the employee merit raise for all qualified full-time classified employees “excluding Department Heads who participate in the Executive Pay Plan”, permanent part-time employees, City Manager, Treasurer, Commissioner of Revenue and the Registrar at 3.5 percent effective July 1, 2015. Department Heads participate in a separate pay plan and are excluded from set merit raises. (In favor, Councilmembers DeMarco, Greenfield, Meyer, Miller, and Schmidt; opposed, Councilmember Loftus.)

 

Approved, unanimously, to approve the health benefit contribution rates for the City of Fairfax and eligible employees for fiscal year 2016 as specified in Attachment D-2 and further move that the City Manager is directed to implement the employee benefits as provided for and described in the adopted fiscal year 2016 budget.

 

Approved, 4-2, to set the real estate tax rate for the 2015 tax year at $1.052 per one hundred dollars of assessed value, of which $.02 per one hundred dollars of assessed value shall be dedicated to the Stormwater Fund, and $0 of one cent per one hundred dollars of assessed value shall be dedicated to the School Tuition Reserve. . (In favor, Councilmembers DeMarco, Greenfield, Meyer, and Miller; opposed, Councilmembers Loftus and Schmidt.)

 

Approved, unanimously, to set the personal property and machinery and tools tax rate for the 2015 tax year at $4.13 per one hundred dollars assessed valuation based upon the assessment ratio of one hundred percent of fair market value.

 

Approved, unanimously, to set the personal property tax rate for the 2015 tax year at $.01 per one hundred dollars assessed valuation for those members of volunteer fire departments or volunteer rescue squads qualifying under the City Code and set the personal property tax rate of $0 for qualifying disabled veterans pursuant to Virginia Code Section 58.1-3219.5.

 

Approved, unanimously, the budget for the Wastewater System incorporating any changes made earlier in this meeting.

 

Approved, unanimously, to adopt the proposed FY 2016 Schedule of Rates and Levies, incorporating any changes made earlier in this meeting.
