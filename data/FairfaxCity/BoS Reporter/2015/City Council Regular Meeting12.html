                     COUNCIL ACTIONS OF

 1.  Call the Regular Meeting to order.
 
 2.  Invocation (Councilmember Loftus).
 
 3.  Pledge of Allegiance.
 
 4.  Presentations and Proclamations.

a.

Presentation of a proclamation declaring Friday April 24, 2015, Arbor Day in the City of Fairfax.

 

b.

Presentation of a proclamation declaring Wednesday, April 22, 2015 as Earth Day and in conjunction with that, Saturday, April 25, 2015 as Spring Clean-up Day in the City of Fairfax.

 

c.

Presentation of a proclamation acknowledging the 30th Anniversary of Spotlight on the Arts.

 
 
 5.  Presentations by the public on any item not subject to a public hearing.
 
 6.  Adoption of the agenda.
 
 7.  Consent agenda.

a.

Introduction of an ordinance amending and readopting Chapter 90, Article II, Division 1, Section 90-33 of the Code of the City of Fairfax, Virginia, pertaining to classification and taxation of certain real property in the City of Fairfax used for or zoned for commercial and industrial purposes, and to establish a separate tax rate on such real property effective as of January 1, 2015, revenues from which would be used exclusively for transportation purposes that benefit the City of Fairfax.  (Public hearing April 29, 2015.)

 

b.

Introduction of an ordinance amending Section 102-31 of the code of the City of Fairfax, Virginia, to increase the Wastewater Utility rate by 10% for Fiscal Year 2015-2016.  (Public hearing April 29, 2015.)

 
Approved, unanimously, the consent agenda.  (Councilmembers DeMarco and Greenfield were absent.)
 
 
 
 8.  Public hearings.

a.

Public hearing on the proposed FY 2015-2016 advertised real estate tax rate of $1.065 per $100 of assessed value, a $0.025 increase from the current city adopted rate of $1.04. The difference between the equalized tax rate and the proposed tax rate would be $0.04 per $100, or 3.90 percent. This difference will be known as the "effective tax rate increase." Individual property taxes may, however, increase at a percentage greater than or less than the above percentage. 
*Tax rate to be set on April 29, 2015*

 
No action required.
 
 
 
 9.  Items not requiring a public hearing.  None.
 
 10.  Approval of minutes.

a.

March 24, 2015 Regular Meeting.

 
Approved, unanimously, the regular meeting minutes of March 24, 2015.  (Councilmembers DeMarco and Greenfield were absent.)
 
 
 
 11.  Closed meeting to discuss appointments to Boards and Commissions and for consultation with legal counsel.
 
 12.  Actions out of Closed Meeting/Appointments to Boards and Commission.

Approved, unanimously, to confirm the appointment of Steve Moloney to the Retirement Plan Administrative Committee as the Alternate Retiree Representative, as elected by the current retirees, for a three-year term commencing on March 1, 2015 through February 28, 2018.  (Councilmen DeMarco and Greenfield were absent.)

 

Approved, unanimously, to appoint Janet Sievert to the Commission for Women for the remainder of a three-year term extending through January 8, 2016, with an extension of a three-year term extending through January 8, 2019.  (Councilmen DeMarco and Greenfield were absent.)

 

Approved, unanimously, to appoint Patricia Gallagher to the Commission on the Arts for a three-year term extending through April 14, 2018.  (Councilmen DeMarco and Greenfield were absent.)
