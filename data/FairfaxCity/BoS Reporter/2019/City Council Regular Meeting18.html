                     COUNCIL ACTIONS OF
                         November 12, 2019 
 
 1.  Call the regular meeting to order.
 
 2.  Invocation (Councilmember Yi).
 
 3.  Pledge of Allegiance.
 
 4.  Presentations and Proclamations.

a.

Presentation of a proclamation acknowledging November 30, 2019 as Small Business Saturday in the City of Fairfax.

 
 
 5.  Presentations by the public on any item not subject to a public hearing.
 
 6.  Adoption of the agenda.
Approved, unanimously the adoption of the agenda.
 
 
 
 7.  Consent agenda.

a.

Consideration of an award of contract to Meadville Land Service, Inc. in an estimated amount of $794,625 for the Tusico Branch of Accotink Creek Stream Restoration Project, Phase I.

 
Approved, unanimously, the consent agenda 7a, to approve the suggested motion in the staff report.
(Councilmember Stehle was absent.)
 
 
 
 8.  Public hearings.

a.

Public hearing and Council action on a supplemental appropriation resolution in the maximum amount of $12,000 to Fair Lakes Youth Association ($7,500), Yellow Ribbons United ($2,000) and The Josh Anderson Foundation ($2,500).

 
The Mayor called for a vote on the motion, which carried unanimously by voice vote with 5-0.
(Councilmember Stehle was absent.) 
 
 

b.

Public hearing and Council action on a request of the City of Fairfax School Board, applicant, by Dr. Phyllis Pajardo, agent, for a Special Use Permit pursuant to City Code Section 110-3.3.1 to allow temporary expansion of Fairfax High School using modular temporary classrooms in the RM Residential Medium Zoning on the premises known as 3501 Rebel Run and more particularly described as Tax Map Parcel 58-1-02-003.

 
Approved, unanimously 5-0 to move to approve the request of the City of Fairfax School Board, applicant, by Dr. Phyllis Pajardo, agent, for a special use permit pursuant to City Code Section 110-3.3.1 Schools, Elementary, Middle, High to allow temporary expansion of Fairfax High School using modular temporary classrooms in the RM Residential Medium Zoning District and Architectural Control Overlay District on the premise known as 3501 Rebel Run and more particularly described as tax map parcel 58-1-02-003, subject to the following condition:
  1. This Special Use Permit (SUP) shall be valid for a period of five (5) years.
(Councilmember Stehle was absent.)
 
 

c.

Public hearing and Council action on request of the City of Fairfax School Board, applicant, by Dr. Phyllis Pajardo, agent, for a Special Use Permit pursuant to City Code Section 110-3.3.1 to allow temporary expansion of Providence Elementary School using modular temporary classrooms in the RM Residential Medium Zoning on the premises known as 3616 Jermantown Rd and more particularly described as Tax Map Parcel 47-3-01-040.

 
Approved, 4-1, to move the request of the City of Fairfax School Board, applicant, by Dr. Phyllis Pajardo, agent, for a special use permit pursuant to City Code Section 110-3.3.1 Schools, Elementary, Middle, High use to allow temporary expansion of Providence Elementary school using modular temporary classrooms in the RM Residential Medium Zoning on the premises known as 3616 Jermantown Road and more particularly described as tax map parcel 47-3-01-040, subject to the following condition:

1. This Special Use Permit (SUP) shall be valid for a period of five (5) years.

(Councilmember Stehle was absent.)
 
 

d.

Public hearing and Council action on an ordinance proposing to grant a franchise to occupy and use the public rights-of-way of the City for the term and on the terms and conditions stated therein, for the construction, maintenance and operation therein of a system of pipes, mains, valves, manholes, connections, meters and other equipment and appliances necessary or convenient for the transmission, distribution and sale of natural gas within the corporate limits of the City.

 
Approved, unanimously to move to enact a draft ordinance granting a non-exclusive franchise to occupy and use the public rights-of-way of the city for the purpose of construction, maintenance and operation of a system of pipes, mains, valves, manholes, connections, meters and other equipment and appliances necessary or convenient for the transmission, distribution and sale of natural gas within the corporate limits of the city as the same now exist or may hereafter be extended or altered.

Further move to award the franchise to Washington Gas Light Company, the successful and selected bidder, and to authorize the Mayor to execute the final ordinance once the required information is inserted into the ordinance, subject to the concurrence of the City Attorney.

(Councilmember Stehle was absent.)
 
 

e.

Public hearing of a consideration of the City of Fairfax 2020 Legislative Program.

 

f.

Public hearing and Council action on an appropriation resolution in the amount of $99,910 to fund an Organizational and Staffing Analysis.

 
Approved, unanimously to approve an appropriation resolution in the amount of $99,910 to fund an organizational and staffing analysis.
(Councilmember Stehle was absent.)
 
 
 
 9.  Items not requiring a public hearing.

a.

Consideration of an award of contract to Catoctin Consulting LLC in the amount of $99,910 for an Organizational and Staffing Analysis.

 
Approved, unanimously to move to authorize the City Manager to execute a contract with Catoctin Consulting LLC in the amount of $99,910 to provide an organizational and staffing analysis, provided the contract and any amendments are acceptable to the City Manager and approved as to form by the City Attorney.
(Councilmember Stehle was absent.)
 
 

REMOVED b. Consideration to receive and file the FY19 City of Fairfax Certified Annual Financial Report (CAFR).

 
 
 10.  Approval of minutes. None.
 
 11.  Council Comments.
 
 12.  Recess the regular meeting to go into work session.

a.

Discussion of the Old Town Service District.

 

b.

Discuss preliminary FY 2019 Financial Results, FY 2020 Budget Update and Suggested FY 2021 Budget Guidelines.

 
 
 14.  Reconvene the regular meeting.
 
 15.  Closed meeting to discuss appointment to Boards and Commissions, for personnel matters, and for consultation with legal counsel for legal litigation and legal advice.
 
 16.  Actions out of closed meeting/appointments to Boards and Commissions.
Appointed Deborah Crawford to Economic Development Authority for a four-year term extending through November 11, 2023.
Appointed Kathleen Paley to Economic Development Authority for a four-year term extending through November 11, 2023.
Reappointed Deborah Dillard to Commission on the Arts for a three-year term extending through December 31, 2022.
Reappointed Anita Light to Human Service Committee for a three-year term extending through October 22, 2022. 
 
 
Approved, unanimously to authorize and approve the settlement of U.S. Bank National Association and C-III (“C3”) Asset Management, LLC v. City of Fairfax (Fairfax County Circuit Court No. CL2019-00924), relating to tax assessment appeals for certain commercial real property and improvements owned by the petitioners, on the terms and conditions outlined by the City Attorney in closed meeting. 
 
 
 
 17.  Adjourn the regular meeting.
