a.

Public hearing and Council action on a request from Theresa Stephens, applicant, for approval of a Special Use Permit, pursuant to City Code Section 110-3.5.5 to allow a Family Home Daycare with 5 to 12 children in the RM, Residential Medium Zoning District on the premises known as 3409 Park Hill Pl and more particularly described as Tax Map Parcel 57 2 10 236. (Deferred from July 9, 2019.)

 
Approved, unanimously, to move that the City Council approve the request from Theresa Stephens, Applicant, for approval of a Special Use Permit, pursuant to City Code Section 110-3.5.5 to allow a family Home Daycare with 5 to 12 children in the RM, Residential Medium Zoning District on the premises known as 3409 Park Hill Pl and more particularly described as tax map parcel 57 2 10 236, subject to the following conditions:
  1. The applicant shall care for a maximum of up to seven children;
  2. The hours of operation shall be limited 6:00 am to 6:00 pm, Monday through Friday;
  3. The outdoor play area shall not be used for playing prior to 8:00 AM;
  4. The driveway shall be maintained for parking of two automobiles for the residential use and one for the family home daycare use;
  5. No signage pertaining to the child care business shall be permitted on the premises;
  6. There shall be no modifications to the exterior of the building which detract from the residential character of the site;
  7. The Special Use Permit shall be valid only for the applicant and shall not be transferable to any other person; and
  8. The applicant shall obtain and maintain all licenses required by the State to operate.

c.

Public hearing and Council action on the consideration of applications for federal Transportation Alternatives Program funding for two projects: construction of a new sidewalk on Sager Avenue just east of University Drive and implementation of bike share.  The Transportation Alternatives Program provides federal funding for projects that expand non-motorized travel choices.  Conceptual design and information about the projects will be available for review and there will be opportunity for public comment about the proposed funding applications.

 
Approved, unanimously, to move to approve a resolution endorsing the submission of two grant applications to the Transportation Alternative Program to fund the Sager Avenue Sidewalk and Bike Share Infrastructure.
