                     COUNCIL ACTIONS OF
                         April 9, 2019 
 
 1.  Call the regular meeting to order.
 
 2.  Invocation (Councilmember Yi).
 
 3.  Pledge of Allegiance.
 
 4.  Presentations and Proclamations.

a.

Presentation of the Chocolate Lovers Festival Grants.

 

b.

Presentation of a proclamation acknowledging April 7-13, 2019 as National Library Week in the City of  Fairfax.

 
 
 5.  Presentations by the public on any item not subject to a public hearing.
 
 6.  Adoption of the agenda.
 
 7.  Consent agenda.

a.

Introduction of an ordinance amending Chapter 102, Article II, Section 102-31 of the Code of the City of Fairfax, Virginia to increase the wastewater utility rate by 10% for FY20.

 

b.

Introduction of an ordinance amending and readopting Chapter 90, Article II, Division 1, Section 90-33 of the Code of the City of Fairfax, Virginia, pertaining to classification and taxation of certain real property in the City of Fairfax used for or zoned for commercial and industrial purposes, and to establish a separate tax rate on such real property effective as of January 1, 2019, revenues from which would be used exclusively for transportation purposes that benefit the City of Fairfax.

 

c.

Introduction of a supplemental appropriation resolution in the amount of $260,875 for the City’s required local contribution into Virginia’s WMATA Capital Fund.

 

d.

Consideration of a resolution confirming the City Manager’s authority to sign standard agreements between the City and the Virginia Department of Transportation (VDOT) and affirming commitment to fund the local share of the City’s projects under agreement with the Virginia Department of Transportation.

 

e.

Consideration of a resolution establishing a Downtown Visioning Committee.

 

f.

Consideration of an award of contract to A&M Concrete Corporation in the amount of $395,000 for the Main Street/Pickett Shopping Center Improvements.

 

g.

Consideration of a task order in the amount of $293,955.82 to RJN Group for continuation of the Sanitary Sewer Evaluation Program (SSES).

 

h.

Consideration of a task order in the amount of $119,842 to RJN Group for Phase 3 of the Geographic Information System (GIS) Updates and Hydraulic Model Development for the City’s sanitary sewer system.

 

i.

Consideration to receive the list of delinquent taxes in compliance with State Code Section 58.1-3921 and write-off taxes in compliance with State Code Section 58.1-3940 and consider the publication of the list of delinquent personal property taxes of more than $100 and delinquent real estate taxes.

 

Approved, unanimously, the consent agenda items, with amendments to #e.

No Council action was taken.

 16.  Actions out of Closed Meeting/Appointments to Boards and Commissions.
  • Appointed Kim Luckabaugh to the Parks and Recreation Advisory Board for a three-year term extending through April 9, 2022.
  • Appointed James Feather to the Planning Commission for a four-year term extending through April 9, 2023 and conditional upon the appointee satisfying all legal requirements for filling/assuming the position, including but not limited to, completing the mandatory statement of economic interests and filing it with the City Clerk, within five (5) business days of the appointment. If the appointee fails or refuses to timely complete the statement of economic interests, the appointment will automatically be rescinded and the position will be filled again according to City Council procedures.
  • Appointed Amir Eftekhari to the Planning Commission for a the remainder four-year term extending through December 31, 2019, with an extension of a four-year term extending through December 31, 2023 and conditional upon the appointee satisfying all legal requirements for filling/assuming the position, including but not limited to, completing the mandatory statement of economic interests and filing it with the City Clerk, within five (5) business days of the appointment. If the appointee fails or refuses to timely complete the statement of economic interests, the appointment will automatically be rescinded and the position will be filled again according to City Council procedures.
