 8.  Public hearings.

a.

Public hearing and Council action on a request of Earp’s Ordinary LLC, applicant, by Michael J. DeMarco, agent, for a Special Use Permit (SU-19-00412) pursuant to City Code Section 110-3.5.5.C to allow dancing and entertainment as an accessory use to a restaurant in the CR Commercial Retail Zoning District and the Old Town Fairfax Historic Overlay District on the premises known as 10426 Main Street and more particularly described as Tax Map Parcel 57-4-02-101.

 
Approved, unanimously move that the City Council approve the request of Earp's Ordinary LLC, applicant, by Michael J. DeMarco, agent, for a Special Use Permit pursuant to City Code Section 110-3.5.5.C to allow dancing and entertainment as an accessory use to a restaurant in the CR Commercial Retail Zoning District and the Old Town Fairfax Historic overlay district on the premises known as 10426 Main Street and more particularly described as Tax Map Parcel, 57-4-02-101, subject to the following conditions:
  1. Operation of this establishment shall be consistent with application, business plan, and floor plan.
  2. All local and state licenses and permits shall be maintained and kept current.
  3. Live musical performances shall end by 1:00 a.m.
  4. Dancing activities shall be limited to the 750-square-foot area shown on the floor plan in front of the stage, and this area shall be clearly demarcated by use of a different floor material, texture, or color.
  5. Maximum patron occupancy shall be limited to 290 persons during live musical performances and events.
  6. The Special Use Permit shall be valid for a period of two years from the date of issuance of a commercial use and occupancy permit. Prior to expiration, the applicant shall apply for renewal of the Special Use Permit application or it shall be deemed null and void.
(Councilmember DeMarco recused from the agenda item, and Councilmember Yi abstained from vote.)
